## **1\. THE BEGINNING \- THE PACT**

### **Time: Unknown Eons Ago**

**Alaryx'dormu \- The Father of Dragons**

* Ancient progenitor dragon, as old as the universe itself  
* Last of his kind, wandering the cosmos  
* **Could not procreate** \- his species dying  
* Desperate, alone, searching for solution

**Zenshu (全宗 \- "Complete Origin") \- The Young God**

* Ambitious young deity building a new world  
* Creating nascent realm from nothing  
* Needs inhabitants and guardians  
* **Method: Kidnapping babies/young from other planes**  
  * Takes from Material Plane, Feywild, other worlds  
  * Doesn't understand mortal emotions or consent  
  * Genuinely believes he's "saving" them  
  * "They'll have better lives in my paradise\!"

**The Meeting**

* Alaryx'dormu discovers Zenshu's young world  
* Two desperate beings find each other  
* Form genuine friendship

**The Sacred Pact**

* **Zenshu's Offer:** "I'll restore your fertility. Give you the ability to hatch eggs. You and your children can live peacefully in my world."  
* **Alaryx's Duty:** "In exchange, help me protect this world I'm creating."  
* **Alaryx Accepts:** Only way to save his species  
* Zenshu uses divine power to restore dragon fertility

**The Six Eggs**

* Alaryx'dormu lays six blessed eggs  
* Each egg imbued with different divine aspect  
* The hatching of the Six Original Dragons

## **2\. THE GOLDEN AGE \- SIX CLANS RISE**

### **Time: Countless Millennia of Peace**

**The Six Original Dragons (Alaryx'dormu's Children)**

**1\. INDRATH (Fey Dragon) \- The Firstborn**

* **Divine Purpose:** Bring order and govern justly  
* Radiant golden scales, time and light magic  
* Becomes ruling bloodline  
* Strongest and wisest

**2\. VRITRA (Serpent Dragon)**

* **Divine Purpose:** Advance society through knowledge  
* Sleek Asian-style serpentine dragon  
* Brilliant scientists, alchemists, researchers  
* **Physically weakest, intellectually strongest**  
* Lazy by nature, prefer laboratories to combat

**3\. GARUDA (Basilisk Dragon)**

* **Divine Purpose:** Protect world and Zenshu's inhabitants  
* Proud warrior, petrification powers  
* Honor-bound, fierce military might  
* Defenders of the realm

**4\. TATTVA (Hydra Dragon)**

* **Divine Purpose:** Guide souls through reincarnation cycle  
* Multi-headed, regenerating  
* Soul magic, death powers, mystical  
* Custodians of River of Souls

**5\. ARANYA (Dragon Tortoise)**

* **Divine Purpose:** Protect and cultivate nature  
* Ancient, patient, healing magic  
* Maintain ecological balance  
* Peaceful guardians of natural world

**6\. SOMARA (Phoenix Dragon)**

* **Divine Purpose:** Bridge mortals with their god  
* Eternal flame dragons of rebirth  
* Cyclical existence (die and reborn in fire)  
* **Zenshu's chosen heralds and voice**  
* Divine intermediaries, perform miracles  
* Orange/red/gold flames (distinct from Indrath's pure light)  
* Immortal through rebirth cycle

**The World Flourishes**

* Six dragons work with Zenshu to maintain balance  
* Each fulfills divine role perfectly  
* Alaryx'dormu watches his children thrive  
* True partnership between god and dragons

**The Second Generation Begins**

* Original six begin procreating (Zenshu's gift works\!)  
* Their offspring inherit aspects and purposes  
* **The Clans form naturally through generations:**  
  * Indrath Clan (governance, order)  
  * Vritra Clan (science, advancement)  
  * Garuda Clan (warriors, protection)  
  * Tattva Clan (soul guides, mystics)  
  * Aranya Clan (nature guardians)  
  * Somara Clan (priests, divine connection)

**Other Mortal Races**

* Kidnapped mortals and their descendants populate world  
* Humans, elves, orcs, various races  
* Live alongside dragon clans  
* Most born here, know no other home  
* Kidnapping becomes distant history

## **19\. Alaxian greetings and ritual phrases**

# **ALAXYAN GREETINGS, ANSWERS, AND RITUAL PHRASES**

*Cultural language conventions of the Sovereign's Empire. Use these as canon for all Alaxya-set scenes, NPC dialogue, and ritual liturgy.*

---

## **OVERVIEW**

Under Nivar'Theon's thousand-year rule, daily speech across Alaxya has been shaped by state religion. Every common Alaxyan, regardless of region or House, uses a set of mandatory call-and-response phrases woven into ordinary social transactions \- receiving goods, agreeing with a statement, parting ways, blessing a journey.

These phrases are not optional. They are reflexive. A native Alaxyan uses them the way a person might say "bless you" after a sneeze \- without thinking, without belief required, simply because their absence is *noticed*.

**For outsiders:** Failure to use the correct response when one is expected immediately marks the speaker as foreign, suspicious, or possibly Dross. Escudo agents are trained to listen for these gaps. Merchants, innkeepers, and dock workers will register the omission even if they don't openly comment on it.

---

## **EVERYDAY CALL-AND-RESPONSE**

These are the six core phrases. They function as social glue and ideological enforcement at the same time.

| Phrase | When Used | Notes |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **"By the Sovereign's grace"** | When receiving anything \- food, coin, lodging, a favor, news | The expected acknowledgment of any transaction. Withholding it is the equivalent of refusing to say "thank you" in a culture that requires it. |
| **"By his blood"** | Affirmation, agreement | Closest equivalent to "indeed," "amen," or "truly." Used to agree with statements about the Sovereign, the Houses, or the order of things. |
| **"His blood flows"** | Casual short form | Everyday shorthand. Used as a passing greeting or quick acknowledgment between strangers in markets, on the road, in line for Anima rations. |
| **"Until the Sovereign summons"** | Farewell | Standard parting. Carries the implicit reminder that all service is owed and all lives belong to him. |
| **"Vritra's scales weigh your deeds"** | Warning, "be careful," veiled threat | A pious-sounding caution. Can be earnest (a worried friend) or pointed (an Escudo agent telling someone to mind themselves). |
| **"May your service bring glory"** | Blessing when parting, especially to someone undertaking a task or journey | Given to travelers, soldiers, Carving candidates, and anyone going to perform duty. The most "warm" of the phrases, though still framed around service. |

### **Usage examples (NPC patterns)**

* **Innkeeper handing over a key:** *"Your room, traveler. By the Sovereign's grace."* Expected response: *"By his blood."*  
* **Two villagers passing on the road:** One nods. *"His blood flows."* The other nods back. *"His blood flows."*  
* **A merchant warning the party away from a topic:** *"Vritra's scales weigh your deeds, friend. Best to drink your ale and move on."*  
* **An Escudo officer dismissing a subordinate:** *"Until the Sovereign summons."*  
* **A mother to her son leaving for the Carving:** *"May your service bring glory."*

---

## **RITUAL CALL-AND-RESPONSE (CARVING LITURGY, TEMPLE SERMONS)**

Formal religious settings use a longer, more theatrical structure. The officiant speaks; the crowd answers. The cadence is deliberately Handmaid's Tale \- rhythmic, communal, hypnotic, drilled into people from childhood.

These exchanges appear during Carving ceremonies, Anima distributions, public sentencings, and Nivar'theon-aligned sermons.

| Officiant | Crowd Response |
| ----- | ----- |
| *"We gather in the presence of the Sovereign."* | *"By his blood, we are sustained."* |
| *"From darkness, he shaped order. From chaos, he forged purpose."* | *"His will is our salvation."* |
| *"Who among you offers themselves to his service?"* | *(a candidate steps forward)* |
| *"Do you offer your flesh freely?"* | *"I offer my flesh freely."* |
| *"Do you accept the blood of the Sovereign into your body?"* | *"I accept his blood."* |
| *"Do you surrender your service to his eternal glory?"* | *"I surrender my service."* |
| *"His blood separates the strong from the weak. His mark reveals your true purpose."* | *"Vritra's scales weigh your deeds."* |
| *"May his blood find you worthy."* | *"May his blood flow through you."* |
| *"For the glory of the Sovereign."* | *"His blood flows eternal."* |

---

## **VARIATIONS AND REGIONAL NOTES**

**The Heartlands and Nis'starya:** The phrases are used most strictly here. Deviation is dangerous. Children are corrected immediately.

**Forlorn and the White Barrens:** Used reflexively but often quietly. The Indrathi descendants carry shame for the Dawnfall and tend to mumble the phrases rather than declaim them. An attentive listener can hear the difference.

**The Dross:** Use the phrases bitterly, ironically, or refuse them entirely depending on how broken or how defiant they are. A Dross who uses the phrases with full sincerity is performing for safety; a Dross who pointedly omits *"By his blood"* in response to a stranger is signaling something \- either trust or recklessness.

**Sylvaren of Thal'myra (House Sylvanas):** Use the Sovereign phrases in public but maintain older, quieter exchanges among themselves rooted in their secret Matron of Ravens worship. *Specifics to be developed when the party arrives at Thal'myra.*

**The Espada and high nobility:** Use the phrases when politically necessary but among each other speak more directly. Excessive use of the formal phrases in private company can be read as either piety or sycophancy depending on who is listening.

**Renn Calder:** As an Abyssal entity wearing a merchant disguise, he has drilled the phrases and uses them fluently. Under stress \- real fear, surprise, or pain \- he may fumble one, swap two, or freeze. A perceptive party member could catch this.

**Ulavor (post-possession):** The entity inhabiting him does not know these phrases natively. Its first attempts to mimic them may be slightly off \- wrong cadence, archaic phrasing, or a response one beat too slow. A potential tell for the party.

---

## **NARRATIVE USAGE GUIDE**

**For the DM at the table:**

* Have NPCs use these phrases constantly in early Alaxya scenes. Saturation establishes the cultural weight.  
* When the party fails to respond correctly, **do not explain it to them.** Let an NPC pause, narrow their eyes, look the speaker up and down, then continue the transaction with new caution. The players should *feel* the social misstep before they understand it.  
* Once players begin using the phrases correctly, certain NPCs (merchants, low-ranking guards, common folk) will visibly relax around them. This is one of the practical rewards of cultural integration.  
* The phrases are also a useful tool for showing internal conflict. An NPC who hesitates before saying *"By his blood"* is telling you something about their faith without saying it aloud.

# **The Shattered Fragment**

Alaxya is not merely a region of the Shadowfell \- it is a broken fragment of it. Long ago, the Shadowfell shattered into countless pieces, each drifting in the void between planes. One fragment was blessed when the god Zenshu chose it as his domain, giving it life and light where only darkness had existed before.

For millennia, Zenshu served as both sun and deity to the dragon clans who inhabited this fragment. His light sustained the leylines, nurtured the land, and gave hope to all who lived beneath his radiance. Then came the Dawnfall \- approximately 1300 years ago \- when Zenshu was slain, and eternal twilight fell upon Alaxya.

Now the realm exists under the tyrannical rule of Nivar'Theon, the Sovereign, who manipulated the clans into killing their own god and has rewritten history to hide his crimes.

**The Dark Beyond**

Beyond Alaxya's borders lies the Dark Beyond \- complete magical darkness and void. It is the edge of the world in every direction, shrouded in impenetrable blackness. Nothing that enters the Dark Beyond returns alive. No one approaches it willingly. It is oblivion made manifest, a constant reminder that Alaxya floats alone in an infinite sea of nothing.

# **Celestial Nobility**

The Vritra are not a Noble House \- they ARE divine nobility. Like the Celestial Dragons of legend, they exist on a completely different level than the 13 Houses. Vritra blood is above all and directly beneath the Sovereign himself.

Approximately 6-7 Vritra Purebloods remain, the last survivors of the original 67 who aided Nivar'Theon after the Dawnfall. They have lost the full power of demigodhood but remain overwhelmingly powerful by blood alone \- essentially devas or demigods in mortal form.

**Celestia \- The Divine Territory**

The Vritra reside in Celestia, a mountain ridge on the northwest side of the Sovereign's castle (TBD \- castle name). Their mansions are completely disconnected from the rest of Nis'starya \- only slaves work for them, and common folk never set foot in their territory.

From Celestia, the Vritra look down upon the entire realm \- both literally and figuratively. They are worshipped, feared, and utterly untouchable. Slaves sent to serve the Vritra \- whether Dross or condemned criminals (even the innocent) \- face a fate worse than death.

## **House Descriptions**

Each house grants unique powers to their chosen champions in the gladiatorial pits:

**House Queth'moor \- The Eternal Flame**

Masters of fire and passion, led by Lord Ashran Queth'moor, 'The Everember,' a noble of few words whose burning eyes revealed nothing. Fire Genasi nobility who trace their lineage to the ancient Garuda clan.

*Location: Southwest Heartlands \- Castle with surrounding territories*

**House Kaminari \- Storm's Fury**

Nobles who bestow lightning reflexes and the power of storm-calling upon their warriors. Water Genasi who command the tempests and tides, descendants of the Garuda clan.

*Location: Inazuma Bluffs (Eastern region, closer to the clouds)*

**House Kaldraxxis \- The Bone Garden**

Nobles whose ancient earth magic grants their champions stone-hard flesh and the ability to summon fossilized terrors from the depths below. Earth Genasi nobility, Garuda descendants.

*Location: Southwest Heartlands border, near Drustwald*

**House Malakorth \- The Void Whispers**

Nobles offering their champions unparalleled stealth and the ability to manifest nightmares. Air Genasi who have embraced the darkness between, commanding void and shadow.

*Location: The Underdark of Alaxya (TBD \- specific region)*

**House Sylvanas \- The Wild Hunt**

Nobles offering the gift of primal transformation and the ability to command nature's wrath. Wood Elves who secretly worship the Matron of Ravens, ruled by High Lady Venatrix Ly'leth from Thal'myra. Ly'leth is the identical twin of Aelindra (Lilith's mother).

*Location: Thal'myra (Oasis in the Ashenwoods) \- Capital: Galanthir, Baby World Tree: Galadrissil*

**House Indrath \- The Golden Eye**

Nobles providing their champions with scales of protection and powerful breath weapons. Ice Elves \- the devolved descendants of the ancient Indrath fey dragons who live in historical shame, believing their clan caused the Dawnfall.

*Location: Sil'varen in the Emberfall Peaks (also Kori'sume village to the east)*

**House Sanguinaire \- The Crimson Court**

An ancient noble house granting their chosen enhanced speed and mastery over blood magic. Dhampir and Vampires who create the Ashborn \- devil-like slaves born from blood rituals.

*Location: Northwest Heartlands (TBD \- specific territory name)*

**House Naz'tharim \- The Black Serpent**

Nobles granting their champions immunity to all poisons and the power of venomous strikes. Devils, Tieflings, and Gargoyles who embrace their infernal heritage.

*Location: Kindlespine (Volcanic mountain region)*

**House Reidan \- The Iron Will**

Nobles bestowing upon their champions unbreakable armor and supernatural weapon mastery. Dwarves and Dragonborn united in martial excellence.

*Location: Heartlands (TBD \- specific territory)*

**House Thrymnir \- The Pale Regency**

Nobles providing their champions with impenetrable frost armor and a devastating freezing breath. Halflings and Gnomes who have mastered cold magic despite their small stature.

*Location: Heartlands \- Small farming fiefdom (low-ranked house)*

**House Vescari \- The Alchemists**

Nobles whose alchemical arts grant their champions astonishing bodily transformation and regeneration. Humans and Downcasts who have risen through scientific mastery and bio-weapon development.

*Location: Nis'starya proper (Capital district)*

**House Gormadran \- The Mountain's Heart**

Nobles whose power turns gladiators into living fortresses. They sponsored Kronn 'The Worldbreaker,' an orc who remained undefeated for three years, his body transformed into living granite. Ogresh and Grudgel \- the devolved Garuda descendants.

*Location: Northeast \- Kor'thani Ridge area*

**House Zan'kali \- The Loa's Hand**

Nobles who commune with ancestral spirits, granting their champions the ability to channel the Loa \- calling upon the dead to guide their strikes and shield their flesh from mortal wounds. Spirit-touched people connected to the Tattva heritage.

*Location: Jum'bala border region*

## **The Heartlands \- Central Empire**

The civilized core of Alaxya, where Nivar'Theon's rule is absolute. Gothic Victorian cities sprawl across the landscape, with Nis'starya as the dark jewel at its heart. Most of the 13 Noble Houses maintain their primary territories within or adjacent to the Heartlands.

**Nis'starya \- The Eternal Capital**

Population: \~500,000. The greatest city of Alaxya, built in concentric rings around the Sovereign's fortress. Dark Gothic architecture of stone, iron, and stained glass. Beneath the city lie the Deep Dungeons where King Arjune and the Dragon Tortoise Ancient have been tortured for approximately 1300 years.

**Celestia \- Vritra Territory**

The mountain ridge northwest of the Sovereign's castle (TBD) where the \~6-7 Vritra Purebloods reside in complete isolation. Their mansions overlook the realm they consider beneath them. Only slaves work here \- Dross and criminals condemned to serve the divine nobility face a fate worse than death.

**The Drumdeep \- River of Souls**

The sacred river that originates from the highest mountain in the Emberfall Peaks (northwest), flowing through ALL the regions of Alaxya before finally spilling under Da'leysen Sanctum in the deep south. Named for the ghostly drum beats that echo from its depths \- the heartbeat of the dead passing through. The water glows silver at night.

## **Northern Reaches**

**Emberfall Peaks \- The Frozen North**

Location: Northwest. Once volcanic mountains of the Indrath fire dragons, now frozen in eternal winter after the Inversion. Time magic corrupts here \- travelers age decades in hours, or find themselves walking the same path endlessly. Dormant volcanoes may still hide beneath the frost, remnants of what the Indrath once were.

The Drumdeep originates here, springing from the highest peak before its long journey south through all of Alaxya.

**Sil'varen \- Indrath Capital**

The ice elf city, home of House Indrath. Beautiful but melancholy, filled with distorted history and the weight of false shame. The Shameful Palace stands as a magnificent but tragic seat of power.

**Kori'sume \- Indrath Village**

An Indrathi village located east of Sil'varen. Smaller settlement maintaining the same shame-based culture of their capital.

**Greyhollow \- Mining Settlement**

A mining settlement on the southern edge of the Emberfall Peaks, closer to the Lowlands. This is where Renn Calder claims to be from.

**The Lowlands \- North Central**

Location: Between Emberfall Peaks and Ashenwoods. Rolling hills, sparse vegetation, perpetual grey skies. Transitional climate \- cold but not frozen.

NO NOBLE HOUSE controls this region. The Lowlands are primarily Dross territories \- when someone fails their Carving and becomes Dross, they are no longer proper citizens and are expected to leave for the outskirts, not taking up space and resources from the Heartlands.

Key Settlement: Fallhaven \- A haven for travelers descending from the mountain passes. Current location of the Unseen Assembly campaign.

**Shattered Coast \- Northeast Coast**

Location: Northeast coastline. Rocky cliffs, fishing villages, cold harbors. Scattered isles offshore where some Laneshi occasionally surface. Primarily Dross territories.

**Kor'thani Ridge \- Mountain Barrier**

Location: Northeast, south of Shattered Coast. 'Spine of Stone' in old Alaric. Natural mountain barrier between the coast and the devastated lands below. Passes through are treacherous.

House: Gormadran (Ogresh/Grudgel)

**Slaglands \- Northeast Valley**

Location: Valley below Kor'thani Ridge, above Scorched Wastes. Named for the slag-like terrain. Perpetual grey, ash drifts from the wastes above. Primarily Dross territories.

**Scorched Wastes \- Ground Zero**

Location: Northeast. Where the god Zenshu died \~1300 years ago. Divine rays still pierce from the sky \- walking in direct light means death. Life survives in shadows and underground.

Inhabitants: Frillers (Titch's people, \~15-20 remaining), Somara survivors (\~10, hidden underground). No noble house \- too dangerous even for Nivar'Theon to control.

**Zhen'kara \- The Godwound**

Location: South/west of Scorched Wastes. 'Godwound' in Alaric. The extended desert/badlands scar from Zenshu's death \- where divine fire scorched outward but didn't maintain the deadly rays. Hot, barren, dangerous. Primarily Dross territories if anyone lives here at all.

**Inazuma Bluffs \- Eastern Bluffs**

Location: East. Coastal bluffs closer to the clouds where storms gather constantly. Lightning strikes the cliffs frequently.

House: Kaminari (Water Genasi)

## **Western Reaches**

**The Ashenwoods \- Overall Region**

Location: West. Corrupted forests where the Aranya dragon turtles devolved into the Drust \- wicker-folk bound to decaying trees. Split between dead forest and living oasis.

**Drustwald \- The Wicker Forest**

Location: Majority of Ashenwoods. Dead wicker forest proper, where the Drust suffer and hag covens exploit them. The trees seem to watch and whisper.

Key Settlement: Glenbrook \- Village struggling to survive among the wicker

Notable Areas: Hallow's End (deepest corrupted area), Gol Var / Gol Inath (ancient Drust sacred sites), The Wicker Altar (central hag coven location)

**Thal'myra \- The Living Oasis**

Location: Western Ashenwoods. An impossibility \- lush blooming grove in the heart of death. House Sylvanas planted a World Tree seed here and successfully grew it.

Baby World Tree: Galadrissil \- NOT a full-grown World Tree, but a sapling of immense potential. Nivar'Theon knows about it and hates its existence, as it circumvents his Anima wine control. He is waiting for a justified excuse to destroy it.

Capital: Galanthir

Ruler: High Lady Venatrix Ly'leth \- identical twin sister of Aelindra (Lilith's mother)

Culture: Opposed to Nivar'Theon but never dares act openly. Least Anima problems \- the grove provides naturally. Secretly worships the Matron of Ravens.

Espada Connection: Seris Sylvanas (Espada 4, Raven Hunt) \- the pride of House Sylvanas, has military authority and the people's hearts

## **Southern Reaches**

**Kindlespine \- Volcanic Mountains**

Location: South of Ashenwoods. Volcanic mountain range with active vents. 'Kindle' \= fire that still burns, 'spine' \= mountain ridge. Hot, dangerous, magma flows.

House: Naz'tharim (Devils, Tieflings, Gargoyles)

**The Molten Flow \- Volcanic Gorge**

Location: Between Kindlespine and Jum'bala. Hot canyon descent with steam vents and boiling pools. Difficult but necessary passage connecting volcanic region to marsh.

**Jum'bala Marsh \- Overall Region**

Location: South. Vast primordial swamplands stretching to the southern seas. The Tattva hydra dragons devolved into the Mire'Tal \- troll-like beings who practice blood magic and worship Loa spirits.

Scale: \~350 miles west to east (2+ weeks travel)

Culture: Jamaican/Voodoo inspired, vibrant despite darkness, Loa worship central

**Da'Morah \- Entry Zone**

Location: Northern Jum'bala. 'Place of crossing' in Mire'Tal tongue. Where heavy mist never lifts \- transitional zone from other regions into true marsh.

Note: NOIR PARTY PORTAL LANDING ZONE (Session 12\)

**Gloomwade \- Outer Marsh**

Location: Outer Jum'bala. Shallow waters, partially submerged ancient Tattva ruins. Grung territories, trading camps. 'Gloom' \+ 'wade' (shallow water you walk through).

**The Bayou \- Central Blood Marsh**

Location: Central Jum'bala. Heart of Mire'Tal blood magic culture. Dense fungal forests with red-capped mushrooms, trees that 'bleed' crimson sap for rituals. Where the Loa are strongest.

House: Zan'kali (Spirit-touched) \- border presence

**Da'leysen Sanctum \- Temple of Bwonduadi**

Location: Deep south Jum'bala. Named for Bwonduadi's title 'Baron of Da'leysen Gloaming.' Ancient Tattva architecture, pilgrimage destination. Even the fearsome Mire'Tal approach with reverence.

The Drumdeep's Journey Ends Here: The River of Souls, having traveled from the highest peak of the Emberfall mountains through all of Alaxya, finally spills UNDER Da'leysen Sanctum into the unknown depths below.

Ruler: Bwonduadi \- Loa of Soul Exchange, actually an impure Tattva with forgotten hydra dragon heritage

**Mycora Thicket \- Fungal Islands**

Location: Southern island chain. Mushroom-covered islands extending into the sea, connected by shallow waters and boat travel. Alien, bioluminescent, tribal.

**Zan'mari Shoals \- Laneshi Territory**

Location: Northeastern islands/waters. 'Waters of the Ancestors' in old Tattva. Where the Laneshi (aquatic Tattva) have underwater settlements. Surface islands mostly abandoned ruins.

**Deep Waters \- Southern Ocean**

Location: Southern seas. Laneshi primary territory. Underwater cities and settlements. Surface ships avoid the deep areas. The horrible twin-killing culture exists only among Laneshi.

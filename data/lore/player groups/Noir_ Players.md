# **Aurene Mistborn**

**Race:** Satyr \- **Class:** Cleric 4 (Moon Domain) \- **Background:** Far Traveler \- **Faith:** Sehanine (Lady Sehanine, the Moon Weaver) \- **Alignment:** Chaotic Good \- **Age:** 21 \- **Gender:** Female \- **Height:** 162cm \- **Weight:** 130lb \- **Skin:** Fair \- **Eyes:** Light brown \- **Hair:** Mustard-blonde \- **AC:** 17 \- **HP:** 30 \- **Speed:** 35ft \- **Passive Perception/Insight:** 16/16

## **Backstory: Where Hope Meets the Feywild**

Aurene was born in Charis, the hidden hamlet in the Lotusden Greenwood where dragonborn and halflings live in harmony with nature. Her arrival was itself a strange omen \- a satyr child born to parents who shouldn’t exist in such a place, yet somehow belonged perfectly.

Her father, **Calenhad Mistborn**, was a satyr ranger who emerged from the deeper Feywild-touched parts of the Lotusden years ago, fleeing something he never spoke of. The elders of Charis took him in, and he became one of their most devoted protectors, guarding the village from the stranger things that lurk where the Material Plane grows thin.

Her mother, **Firiel Mistborn**, was a summer faerie who fell through a crossing during a particularly vibrant bloom season. She chose to stay after falling in love with Calenhad and the peaceful community, using her knowledge of fey silk-weaving to help the village prosper. She taught the halflings new ways to work with spider silk and moonbeam-touched threads.

Aurene inherited her father’s satyr features, but with an otherworldly twist \- both her hooves and freckles shimmer with an opalescent sheen that changes color with her emotions, and when she laughs (which is often), tiny phantom bells chime in the air around her. The dragonborn children called her “Chimesong,” while the halfling elders saw her bells as Melora’s own laughter.

## **The Temple in the Hollow Tree**

Charis had no formal temple to Sehanine, but in the heart of the village stood an ancient hollow tree called the **Dreaming Oak**. Its interior naturally formed a spiral that reached up toward a gap in the canopy where moonlight poured in. Here, **Elder Thimble Greenleaf**, a halfling druid-priest who served both Melora and Sehanine, recognized Aurene’s unique gift.

When she was seven, golden threads began sprouting from her fingertips whenever she tried to help injured animals or mend torn fishing nets. Elder Thimble, despite being blind, could “see” what others couldn’t \- that Aurene could perceive the threads connecting all living things.

“Every soul has a thread, little lamb,” he’d say, guiding her hands to feel the invisible connections. “In Charis, we understand this better than most. The dragonborn and halflings, the forest and the village, the Material and the Fey \- all are woven together. You, dear child, were born to tend these threads.”

Under his teaching, Aurene learned to see the golden filaments that connected every living being. She could pluck them gently, sending healing warmth or whispered comfort along their lengths. The village children would bring her injured birds and torn clothes alike, and she’d fix both with equal joy.

## **The Night Everything Changed**

For twenty-one years, Aurene lived in perfect happiness, weaving dreamcatchers that she’d hang throughout the village. Each morning, they’d vanish \- taken by Sehanine, she believed, to catch the bad dreams of the forest itself.

But two months ago, the threads began to die.

It started with the **Silverleaf family** \- halflings who lived in the hollow oak by the stream. One morning, they emerged from their home moving wrong. Their movements were perfect, too perfect, like dolls on strings. Their eyes still saw, their mouths still smiled, but their threads \- those golden connections Aurene could always see \- were gone. They continued their daily routines: gathering, cooking, even laughing at the right moments in conversations. But it was all hollow mimicry.

Within a week, it had spread to a quarter of the village. **“The Hollowed”**, as Elder Thimble called them, weren’t violent or dangerous. They were worse \- they were empty. They’d embrace their children with no warmth, speak words of love with no feeling, tend gardens they no longer cared about.

Then it took her parents. Aurene woke to find Calenhad and Firiel standing perfectly still in their home, staring at nothing. When she touched them, they turned to her with empty smiles. “Good morning, daughter,” they said in unison, their voices flat as pond water. They made breakfast. They asked about her day. But their threads \- those beautiful golden strands that had always pulsed with love \- were severed.

She ran to Elder Thimble, sobbing, begging him to fix it. The old halfling held her as she wept, but his blind eyes were full of sorrow. “I cannot reweave what has been cut, little lamb. The threads are gone, not tangled or torn \- gone.”

## **The Desperate Flight**

That night, something came for the rest of Charis. Aurene never saw it clearly \- just shadows that moved wrong, whispers that came from nowhere, and the horrible sound of threads being cut like harp strings snapping. She watched from her hiding spot in the Dreaming Oak as more villagers emerged from their homes, Hollowed and empty.

By dawn, only thirty souls remained with their threads intact. Elder Thimble, tears streaming down his weathered face, made the hardest decision: “We flee. Now.”

The survivors grabbed what they could and ran deeper into the Lotusden, away from their hollow friends and family who continued their morning routines as if nothing had happened. Aurene looked back once to see her parents working in their garden, side by side, silent and empty.

They made camp in a hidden grove three days from Charis. That night, Elder Thimble pulled Aurene aside: “Little lamb, this is magical \- something beyond my understanding. But I’ve heard tales… in the west, across the waters, there are people who study such things. Mages, wizards, those who understand the deep magics. Perhaps… perhaps they know how to restore what was cut.” He pressed a small pouch into her hands \- a few coins, all the survivors could spare. “Follow the river west. Find answers. Find help.”

## **The Awakening of Hunger**

Aurene left that night, following the river westward with nothing but her clothes, her threadwork supplies, and a small bag of berries. For three days, she walked, the berries long gone, her stomach cramping with hunger.

On the fourth day, she found a rabbit caught in an old snare, leg broken, crying in pain. Her healing instincts kicked in \- golden threads already forming at her fingertips to mend the break. But her stomach roared, and something primal awakened. She looked at the rabbit \- really looked at it. Not as a creature to save, but as meat. Food. Survival.

“I’m sorry,” she whispered, tears streaming down her face. “I’m so, so sorry.”

The kill was messy, inexperienced, horrible. She sobbed the entire time. But when she bit into the raw flesh, still warm, something inside her exploded with satisfaction. The taste was iron and life and everything the berries had never been. She ate until her face was covered in blood, crying and laughing simultaneously, thanking Sehanine between desperate bites.

That night, she dreamed the moon had teeth.

## **Nicodranas: A Disaster**

After two weeks of travel (and many more “hunting prayers”), Aurene reached Nicodranas. The port city was beyond her comprehension \- buildings taller than trees, ships like floating houses, and so many people her thread-sight nearly blinded her.

But the smells\! Cooked meat everywhere\! Stalls selling things she’d never imagined \- sausages, beef, mysterious spiced meats on sticks. Her mouth watered uncontrollably. She approached a vendor, pointed at everything, and started eating.

She didn’t understand why the man started yelling. In Charis, food was shared freely. She didn’t understand the concept of “payment” or “theft.” When the city guards came, she ran, terrified and confused, clutching a half-eaten turkey leg. They chased her to the city gates, shouting words she didn’t understand: “Thief\!” “Pay\!” “Criminal\!” One guard struck her shoulder with a club. She fled into the wilderness, crying and afraid, deciding that cities were terrible, dangerous places full of angry people who wouldn’t share food.

## **The Journey North & Arrival in Yrrosa**

Aurene stuck to the forests, following deer paths northward, away from the frightening civilization. For two weeks, she lived like an animal \- hunting small creatures, eating them raw while crying and praying, sleeping in trees, avoiding any settlement larger than a few houses. Her clothes became stained and tattered. Her hair grew wild with twigs and her spider Whisper tangled in it. But she grew stronger, leaner, her hunting improving each day. The phantom bells around her had taken on a predatory rhythm.

After nearly a month of heading north, Aurene stumbled into a stretch of barren land. The forests thinned into wide, hungry fields, and the deer trails vanished. With nothing left to hunt, she gnawed on grass and leaves, her body growing thin and frail. When the wind shifted and carried the scent of roasting food, it struck her like a blow.

At last, she saw the clustered rooftops of Yrrosa on the horizon. On its outskirts, a farm sprawled wide, alive with strange birds \- chickens and ducks she had never seen before. Desperation overcame caution. When she thought no one was watching, she snatched a chicken and shoved it into her pouch. But a nearby farmer shouted and gave chase. Terror lanced through her; she thought the nightmare of Nicodranas was about to repeat. Clutching her father’s cloak tight around her, she prayed he hadn’t seen her face.

Breathless and shaking, she pushed on into the town proper. At the gates, crownsguard eyes narrowed at her ragged figure, suspicion heavy in their stares. Rather than risk another confrontation, Aurene slipped away from their path and made straight for the Whispering Stone Inn.

# **Calliope**

**Race:** WuKong \- **Class:** Barbarian 4 (Path of the Infernal) \- **Background:** Gladiator \- **Alignment:** Chaotic Good \- **Gender:** Female \- **Size:** Medium \- **AC:** 13 \- **HP:** 41 \- **Speed:** 35ft \- **Darkvision:** 60ft \- **STR 19 / DEX 15 / CON 12 / INT 8 / WIS 12 / CHA 11**

## **The Tale of Calliope**

### **I: The Skynimbus**

High up in the mountains at the peak of the **Jade Summit**, there lay a small forest, and within it, lived the **Skynimbus** \- a secluded tribe of the elusive Wind Walkers, agile monkey-people, who built their colony up in the trees in an elaborate network of rope bridges and tree houses.

Calliope was eighteen when her father, **Lingfei**, began teaching her the art of wind walking \- a form of dance, meditation, and martial arts. Life was calm, secluded, safe; and in the evenings, by the campfire, the elders told stories of starlit legends and the journey of the Monkey King.

But one fateful night, the red flare in the sky appeared, and all hell broke loose. The raiders came and slaughtered everyone in their way, grabbing every able-bodied wukong and chaining them up in fiery shackles. Calliope watched in terror as her father, the strong, calm man, fell to the ground, staff shattered. **Makari**, Calliope’s best friend, was taken with her, and as they were captured, held her hand with a reassuring smile. A fate worse than death was awaiting them.

### **II: The Pit**

**Headmaster Therone Shadowclaw** was a terrifying sattare, a creature of scale and blood. He was watching them as they arrived, a sinister spark in his eye. They were led through the underground that looked like the hells themselves.

“Tomorrow is your big debut,” the menacing orc that held them snarled as he threw them into a large dungeon cell and locked the bars.

The next day, Makari was taken. The day after was Calliope’s turn. She was led to **The Pit** \- a large arena surrounded by hundreds of cheering creatures of all kinds and sizes, and on the topmost pedestals, sat what she will later recognize as **The Sponsors** \- heads of each House of Power. She fought every day, desperately trying to survive against monsters, fiends, and even some of the other trainees.

### **III: The Houses of Power**

The thirteen patrons of the lucky gladiator chosen by them as champion. **House Gormadran** (The Mountain’s Heart) sponsored Kronn “The Worldbreaker.” **House Sanguinaire** (The Crimson Court) \- blood magic and enhanced speed. **House Queth’moor** \- fire genasi nobility, the house that would eventually claim Calliope.

### **IV: Friends in the Pit**

* **Thessa** \- a centaur who took Calliope under her wing and taught her the ways of the Pit. “You must stay vigilant, and give it your all, or else the houses won’t even glance at you.”

* **Burnt Benzo** \- a human monk who spoke few words, but his burnt skin told of secrets and misery.

* **Marrok** \- a half-orc who has been in the Pit for months, growing more feral with each fight.

* **Rexie** \- a ferocious yet witty kobold, known for her dexterity and cunning.

### **V: The Pit of Despair (Bloodbath)**

The first weekend heralded the Bloodbath \- a showcase of each new recruit before the houses. Calliope’s first match was against a pair of death dogs. She won by crushing one beast’s spine and choking the other with her tail while driving her staff through its heart. Her second match was a water weird. Her third was two human trainees \- she tried to disable, but one produced a hidden shiv and nearly gutted her. Her staff caved in his skull while her tail snapped the other’s neck.

“And for the grand finale, the battle of beasts \- monkey versus horse\! The epic battle between the ties that bind and the ties that release\!” the headmaster declared as Calliope was ushered in to fight Thessa.

In House Queth’moor’s box, **Lord Ashran**’s fingers drummed once, twice, against his throne’s arm. Thessa was willing to do anything for her position; Calliope hesitated to hurt her friend. The centaur charged. There was no other way. Calliope had a chance to kill her friend at the last moment, but hesitated; Thessa roared “There’s no room for weakness” and turned the tables. Pinned under the centaur’s weight, Calliope gathered wind underneath her and flung sand into Thessa’s eyes, maneuvered around her torso, and with one sweeping motion, broke her neck.

The centaur crashed lifeless. As Calliope’s legs buckled and she coughed blood, Lord Ashran stood \- a gesture so rare that other Houses took notice. He pointed at Calliope with one finger that seemed to burn the air itself. With the final crowd roar, Calliope was chosen for House Queth’moor’s sponsorship.

### **VI: The Rite of Flame**

When she regained consciousness, Calliope found herself in new chambers, a proper bed and clean water. But she felt the weight of a metal collar around her neck. Pulls of pain emanated through her body when she tried to remove it. **Brin and Siana**, twin fire elemental servants, prepared her for her new life.

At dinner she devoured every pickled egg and turkey leg, ravenously drinking goblet after goblet. Lord Ashran snapped his fingers; a servant fetched a heated rod bearing the Emblem of House Queth’moor \- a dragon wing in flames. The twins grappled Calliope, knelt her to the floor while a third servant presented the rod to the Lord.

Lord Ashran rose, took the rod, and singed her skin. Her screams echoed through the chamber, but no one came to her aid. “You must kill beautifully,” he whispered as she whimpered. “You must kill memorably. You must kill in ways that make the crowd scream our House’s name. Any less and you will suffer pain that will make this feel like cold weather.” He dropped the rod and left the room.

### **VII: The Fire Within**

The magic from the brand coursed through her veins and imbued her with elemental power. Calliope could now wield it, and use the fire within her to win battle after battle. Her rage became an asset. But the fire began to slowly burn her soul, as if her innate wind was constantly feeding the blaze from within.

Her chance came during a fight with a minotaur. A large explosion in the arena engulfed a quarter of the crowd and some of the trainee cages. The richer patrons began teleporting via portal magic out of the Pit. Calliope, bruised and desperate, ran toward the broken trainee cage to seek Makari. She found him on the floor, still awake. She grabbed him and they both ran toward one of the closing portals. She pushed Makari in first \- and was about to follow when a pain like no other surged through her body. She collapsed as the portal closed behind him. She saw the feet of her captor, holding a strange wand, looking down at her with an angry expression. He kept using it. Another explosion pushed the Lord aside \- a few seconds. Calliope dove down through a stairwell right into another portal, and as it shut behind her, she smelled the sweet scent of freedom.

### **VIII: A New Life**

The teleportation was agonizing. She could feel the collar heating up and searing her neck, until she couldn’t feel anything anymore, except for blades of grass below her. As she slowly lost consciousness, she thought, *if this is where I’m going to die, at least it’s under the blue sky*… when a face entered her peripheral vision \- a blue-skinned boy with bright eyes. The last thing she remembered was his hand reaching toward her.

When she woke up, she was at the **Whispering Stone Inn**, run by **Daria**. “Mezmere took care of those shackles, but that collar… even he couldn’t crack it. It seemed to cause you pain when he tried, so we left it be.”

A week passed since then, and Calliope grew stronger every day, happier even. But the weight of the collar, and Lord Ashran’s words, echoed through her body and mind. Visions of blood and battle haunted her sleep. And she knew in her heart that it was just a matter of time before all of this would be taken away from her, once again.

# **Nelvin**

**Race:** Ashborn (small red-skinned devils with horns and scorpion tails, created by House Sanguinaire via blood ritual) \- **Class:** Gunslinger 4 (High Roller) \- **Background:** Custom \- **Size:** Small \- **AC:** 15 \- **HP:** 33 \- **Speed:** 30ft \- **Darkvision:** 60ft \- **STR 8 / DEX 18 / CON 13 / INT 10 / WIS 10 / CHA 15**

## **Nelvin’s Tale**

The castle grounds of **House Sanguinaire** had been Nelvin’s entire world for nineteen years. The crimson banners fluttering against grey stone walls, the endless corridors reeking of old blood and damp moss \- this was all any ashborn knew. **Master** never showed himself to the likes of Nelvin, remaining a shadowy presence whose will filtered down through the vampire servants like poisoned water trickling through cracks.

The **Ashborn Overseers** \- known as “**Crimson Wardens**” \- were the worst kind of traitor. These ashborn had proven their loyalty so thoroughly that they’d been granted minor authority over their own kind. They wore iron collars etched with the House seal, marking them as Master’s eyes among the workforce. The one who caught Nelvin that fateful day was **Scorch**, a particularly cruel overseer with one broken horn and burn scars covering half his grey-red face. Scorch delighted in finding reasons to report workers to the vampire guards.

**Sire Lucas Kennington**, Nelvin’s direct superior, was a gaunt vampire with paper-thin skin and fingers stained permanently black from gunpowder. He spoke in clipped, precise words and treated his workshop like a cathedral. Every gear, every spring, every grain of powder had its place. He never looked directly at Nelvin, addressing him only as “imp” or “you there.” The Sire’s workshop smelled of oil, sulfur, and the metallic tang that followed vampires everywhere.

When Scorch cornered Nelvin outside the workshop, his yellow eyes gleaming with suspicion, Nelvin’s tail moved instinctively \- a lifetime of quick thinking compressed into one desperate moment. The brass fitting disappeared into the coiled appendage just as Scorch’s clawed hand grabbed his vest. “Wasting my time, are you?” Scorch hissed, his own tail twitching with irritation. He dragged Nelvin to the courtyard where **Madame Corvina**, the head of servant assignments, waited \- ancient even by vampire standards, her skin like yellowed parchment.

“This one for the courier duty,” Scorch said simply. Nelvin’s stomach dropped. Every ashborn knew what courier duty meant \- you left, and you never came back.

## **The Portal and the Sun**

The vampire assigned to the mission was **Lord Matthias Duskweaver**, a lesser noble of House Sanguinaire who carried himself like he deserved better than escort duty. His face was aristocratically sharp, with slicked-back black hair and eyes like chips of obsidian. He barely acknowledged Nelvin’s existence as they prepared to depart through the portal \- a swirling mass of shadow and crimson energy maintained by the House’s warlocks.

Stepping through felt like being turned inside out and frozen simultaneously. Then suddenly \- Wildemount. The Material Plane. Nelvin’s first breath of free air burned his lungs with its purity.

The sun \- oh, the sun\! Nelvin had heard vampires curse it, but seeing it for the first time made his eyes water with its brilliance. Lord Duskweaver immediately retreated to his coffin, leaving Nelvin to guide their black horse along roads that seemed to stretch forever. Trees that weren’t twisted and dying, grass that was actually green, birds that sang instead of shrieked \- every moment was a revelation.

For weeks they traveled, Duskweaver emerging only at night to feed and continue their mysterious mission. He never told Nelvin what they sought, only demanded they keep moving northwest.

## **The Ambush**

The adventuring party that found them consisted of seven members \- Nelvin remembered each face burned into his memory by terror. The leader, a human woman in plate armor bearing the symbol of the Platinum Dragon; a halfling with knives that gleamed with holy water; a wizard whose fingers crackled with divine light. They descended upon the abandoned mill where they’d sheltered like the wrath of the gods themselves.

Nelvin hid in a grain barrel, hands pressed over his mouth to muffle his breathing as Lord Duskweaver shrieked and burned. When silence finally fell, he waited until nightfall before emerging. The coffin was destroyed, Duskweaver was ash, and the horse had fled in terror. For the first time in his life, Nelvin was completely alone.

## **Becoming a Gunslinger**

The months that followed tested every skill he’d never known he had. His precious handgun \- cobbled together from stolen springs and a butter knife hammered into a crude barrel \- actually worked when he needed it. The blunderbuss, ugly as sin and held together with wire and prayer, brought down rabbits and birds. Each successful shot filled him with a pride he’d never experienced in nineteen years of servitude.

When Yrrosa’s chimneys appeared on the horizon, Nelvin nearly wept with relief. The town was everything the castle grounds weren’t \- alive, chaotic, full of colors that weren’t red or grey.

## **Talis Goldscale**

**Talis Goldscale** found him on his third night in town, trying to eat a moldy apple core behind The Copper Kettle tavern. The bronze dragonborn had scales that had lost their luster, one milky eye, and breath that reeked of cheap wine. His three-shell game was older than Exandria itself, but he had a patter that could charm coins from a miser’s purse.

“You’re an ugly little thing, aren’t you?” Talis had said, not unkindly. “But ugly can be useful. How’d you like to earn some real food?”

For six months, Talis was the closest thing to family Nelvin had known. The dragonborn taught him the game, the patter, the art of distraction. That Talis pocketed nine copper of every ten Nelvin earned didn’t matter \- Nelvin was just grateful to belong somewhere, to someone.

The night Talis died, he’d been drinking at The Broken Sword and mouthed off to three members of the **Scar Pack**, a local gang that controlled the eastern docks. They left him bleeding out behind a tannery, and Nelvin found him by following the smell of blood \- a scent no ashborn could mistake. “Take the hat,” Talis wheezed, his one good eye already glazing. “It’s lucky.”

It wasn’t. The hat was moth-eaten, the goggles were cracked, and the vest smelled like a decade of unwashed dragonborn. But Nelvin wore them like armor.

## **The Free Ashborn**

The gnome who ran the shop \- **Thistlework** \- had wild white hair that defied gravity and spectacles with at least six different lenses that could flip down. His shop smelled of gunpowder and possibility. When Nelvin palmed the paper packet of bullets while Thistlework demonstrated the proper loading technique for a pistol Nelvin could never afford, he felt only the slightest twinge of guilt. Survival came first \- another lesson from Talis.

Now Nelvin works the three-shell game on Copper Row, where the merchants aren’t rich enough to hire guards but aren’t poor enough to ignore a rigged game entirely. His ashborn features hidden beneath Talis’s old hat and a scarf wrapped around his lower face, his tail carefully tucked into modified trousers, he’s become just another street hustler in Yrrosa’s ecosystem of survival.

Sometimes, late at night in whatever doorway or alley he’s claimed for shelter, Nelvin takes out his handmade guns and cleans them with a rag that might have once been part of a House Sanguinaire banner. He remembers the weight of chains he never knew he wore until they were gone. He remembers Lord Duskweaver’s ash scattered on a mill floor, and Talis’s blood soaking into cobblestones, and the sun \- always the sun \- burning away the shadows of his old life.

He doesn’t know what he’s becoming, this free ashborn in a world that doesn’t know what to do with him. But for the first time in his life, he gets to find out for himself. And that terrifies and thrills him in equal measure. And doesn’t know how to return home, but perhaps someday he’ll be able to return to the Master.

# **Present Wanderleaf**

**Race:** Verdan (Oni Bloodline manifest) \- **Class:** Sorcerer 4 (Oni Bloodline) \- **Background:** Custom \- **Alignment:** Chaotic Good \- **Skin:** Grey-Blue (jade-green base) \- **Eyes:** Orange \- **Age:** 23 \- **Gender:** Male \- **Height:** 3.5ft / 1m \- **Size:** Small \- **AC:** 10 \- **HP:** 20 \- **STR 7 / DEX 10 / CON 13 / INT 14 / WIS 12 / CHA 18**

## **A Verdan Oni Bloodline Sorcerer’s Tale**

### **The Smallest Verdan**

Present was born in the village of **Jade Hollow**, a small Verdan settlement nestled in the northern reaches of the Ashkeeper Peaks, where the mountains meet the edges of the Dwendalian Empire. Like all Verdan, his people had emerged from the Underdark generations ago, their jade-green skin and black blood marking them as children of chaos, transformed by **That-Which-Endures**.

The village had adopted many human customs from the nearby Empire settlements, though they maintained their wandering spirit and deep suspicion of oppressive laws. Present grew up hearing tales of the great growth \- that transformative moment when a Verdan would suddenly shoot up from goblin-size to their full height. His childhood friends **Wurxee Brightear** and **Kalo Threerings** underwent their growth spurts at seventeen and eighteen respectively, their small frames expanding over painful days until they stood tall and proud.

But Present’s eighteenth year came and went. Then his nineteenth. His twentieth. By twenty-one, he remained barely three and a half feet tall, his jade skin unmarred by the stretching that accompanied growth. The village healer, **Grandmother Takat Moonwhisper**, a kindly Verdan whose own hair had turned silver-white with age, examined him countless times but could find no reason for his stunted form.

### **Years of Torment**

The mockery began subtly. **Dreeda Copperlog**, once his closest friend, started calling him “Presentling” instead of Present. **Stromvo the Bold**, who had grown to nearly six feet and whose skin had darkened to deep emerald, would pat Present on the head like a child whenever they passed. But the worst were the **Grimjaw brothers \- Hulm and Gruvald** \- twin Verdan whose mutations had given them particularly prominent ears that they pierced with iron rings. They had made Present’s life a particular misery.

“Look, it’s the eternal child\!” Hulm would bellow across the market square. “Maybe That-Which-Endures forgot about this one,” Gruvald would add with a cruel laugh. “A Verdan stuck between changes \- neither goblin nor proper Verdan\!”

The village elder, **Sollo Wisebrow**, a Verdan whose forehead had indeed grown large with his intelligence-enhancing mutations, tried to maintain peace. But even he looked at Present with a mixture of pity and unease, as if Present’s condition might be contagious or cursed.

Present learned to avoid the main paths through Jade Hollow, taking longer routes through the surrounding woods to avoid his tormentors. He helped **Varr Gentlehands**, the village’s human-trained blacksmith, with small tasks that required nimble fingers and the ability to fit into tight spaces \- the only advantages his size afforded him.

### **The Chase and the Fall**

On Present’s twenty-second birthday \- a day that should have been celebrated but instead reminded everyone of his continued failure to grow \- the Grimjaw brothers’ harassment reached its peak. They had been drinking **Praet**’s strong mushroom wine at the tavern and spotted Present trying to slip past toward his family’s hut.

“There’s our little goblin-kin\!” Hulm shouted. “Maybe if we stretch him out, he’ll finally grow\!”

The chase led them deep into the Ashkeeper foothills, far from the village boundaries where the ground became treacherous with hidden ravines and unstable shale. Present knew these woods well, but panic made him careless. As he scrambled over a moss-covered log, the ground beneath gave way.

The ravine was narrow but deep \- perhaps thirty feet down. Present’s small body bounced off rocky outcroppings before landing hard on a bed of ancient bones and rubble. His left arm bent at an unnatural angle. The last thing he heard was Gruvald’s voice from above: “Hulm, we… we should go. Tell no one. If they find out we chased him here…”

### **The Hunger and the Discovery**

The first day, Present tried calling for help until his voice went hoarse. His telepathic ability, limited to thirty feet, was useless at this depth. The second day, with his arm throbbing and stomach cramping from hunger, he began exploring. The ravine was little more than a crack in the earth, just wide enough for someone of Present’s stature.

Desperate and delirious from hunger, Present noticed a gap in the ravine wall \- a tunnel barely large enough for him to squeeze through. Using his good arm, he pulled himself inch by agonizing inch through the passage. The tunnel opened into a natural cave system, dimly lit by phosphorescent fungi. Clusters of Underdark mushrooms \- varieties that had grown here since before the Calamity \- provided sustenance, though they tasted of earth and decay.

Deeper in the cave, Present discovered something else: the remains of something ancient and wrong. The corpse was humanoid but wrong \- too large, with tusks jutting from its lower jaw and skin that might once have been blue or red but had long since mummified to grey leather. **An oni**, though Present didn’t know the word. One of the demon-touched beings that had wandered Exandria in ages past, perhaps fleeing into these caves during the Divergence when the gods battled and the world broke.

The hunger was unbearable. The preserved flesh of the oni-thing looked almost like jerked meat in the fungi’s glow. He told himself it was just old leather, just preserved food, just survival. The first bite tasted of copper and ash. The second of power and rage. By the third, something else was eating with him \- something that had been waiting in that corpse for centuries for a suitable host.

### **The Awakening**

The transformation wasn’t immediate. As Present consumed the oni’s flesh, he felt warmth spreading through his broken arm. The bone reset itself with a sickening crack that made him scream. His eyes began to burn, and when he looked at his reflection in a pool of cave water, they shimmered with an otherworldly quality.

More importantly, he felt power. Not the chaos-touched nature of That-Which-Endures, but something older and angrier. When he pressed his hands against the cave wall in frustration, the earth responded. Mold Earth came instinctively, as if the oni’s essence had unlocked something that had always been within him.

It took Present three more days to mold enough earth to create crude handholds up the ravine wall. Three days of feeling the oni’s essence settling into his bones, whispering in languages he didn’t understand but somehow comprehended.

### **The Empty Village**

When Present finally pulled himself from the ravine, exhausted and changed, the sun was setting. He stumbled through the forest toward Jade Hollow, expecting to face questions, anger, perhaps even exile. Instead, he found silence.

The village was destroyed. Not burned or razed, but torn apart as if by great beasts or violent magic. Doors hung from broken hinges. Varr’s forge was cold, his tools scattered. Grandmother Takat’s herb garden was trampled, her careful arrangements crushed into the mud.

Present found signs of struggle everywhere \- claw marks on walls, weapon strikes on doorframes, dark stains that might have been his people’s black blood. Footprints of various sizes led in all directions, as if the villagers had scattered in panic. But there were other prints too \- larger, some clawed, some booted.

In his family’s hut, everything valuable was gone. Only broken furniture and torn clothing remained. On the wall, someone had carved a single word in Goblin: “**FLED**.” Whether it was a message from his family or a cruel taunt from attackers, Present would never know.

### **The Wandering Years and the Road to Yrrosa**

With no home and no people, Present began to walk. He followed the trade roads north toward the Dwendalian Empire. His size made others mistake him for a goblin child, and more than once he had to flee from Imperial guards. He learned to hide his jade skin with mud and rags. He discovered Mold Earth had practical applications \- farmers would pay a few copper pieces to have their fields tilled. **Farmer Henrik Plowman** near Nogvurot let him sleep in the barn. **Marta Seedsower** outside Grimgolir shared her soup.

The oni’s presence grew stronger when he was hungry or angry. Sometimes his eyes would shimmer without his meaning them to. In Berleben, a drunk merchant named **Gavven Goldfoot** tried to capture him \- “goblins fetch good coin in the fighting pits.” Present barely escaped, throwing dirt in the man’s eyes and disappearing into the night.

He heard of Yrrosa from **Tamsin Cartwright**, a traveling merchant who didn’t care that Present looked like a goblin as long as his coin was good. “Farming town. Good folk, mostly. They mind their business if you mind yours. Take the Amber Road west from Berleben, then south at the crossing. Can’t miss it.”

As Present approached Yrrosa, he felt something he hadn’t experienced since before the ravine \- hope. The town spread before him in the afternoon sun. Present pulled his hood lower and entered Yrrosa with three silver pieces in his pocket, an oni’s hunger in his soul, and a desperate need to belong somewhere, anywhere, even if just for a little while.

# **Shimmer Tenmentsu**

**Race:** Etherean \- **Class:** Bard 4 (College of the Road) \- **Background:** Entertainer \- **Size:** Medium \- **AC:** 12 \- **HP:** 20 \- **Speed:** 30ft (35ft on Ethereal Plane) \- **Darkvision:** 60ft \- **STR 8 / DEX 12 / CON 10 / INT 12 / WIS 16 / CHA 19** \- **Cosmic purple skin, silvery-white hair that floats and dissipates, tiny horn buds purple-white gradient, cosmic freckles that twinkle in the dark**

*Her circus name, meaning “blinking” or “flickering” in an old tongue \- given for how she appeared to them, flickering between existence itself.*

## **Part I: The Carnival’s Daughter (812-825 P.D.)**

### **The Discovery (812 P.D.)**

On the Material Plane, near the **Truscan Vale** between the Menagerie Coast and the Marrow Valley, **Cornelius’s Fantastical Cavalcade of Wonders** was setting up for a show. It was **Tumble** the halfling who heard it first \- a baby’s cry that seemed to come from nowhere and everywhere at once.

The circus folk found her lying in the grass, flickering in and out of existence like a candle in the wind. Each time she solidified, she screamed louder. Each time she faded, the world went eerily silent. An ethereal silk blanket was the only constant, existing in both planes simultaneously.

**Maestro Cornelius Brightwheel**, a tiefling with deep purple skin and elegant curved horns, approached slowly. His once-legendary singing voice had been stolen by a curse years ago, leaving him unable to carry a tune, but he could still speak. “She’s magnificent,” he said softly, his voice warm but notably flat.

**Madame Seraphina Moonweaver**, a half-elf divination wizard, knelt beside them. Her magic revealed flashes of gray horror, parental sacrifice, and something else \- a mother with similar horns but different coloring. “She’s a child of two bloods. Two worlds in more ways than one.”

When Cornelius held the infant, she calmed. The baby’s tiny hand reached up to touch his face, her flickering finally steadying. **Old Nan Threeeyes** proclaimed it a sign. “The Child of Crossings has come to those who belong to none.”

They named her **Shimmer** \- for the way she flickered between worlds. The surname **Tenmentsu** came from an old carnival tongue, meaning the blink of an eye, the flicker of light, the moment between moments.

### **The Amulet (813 P.D.)**

For months, the circus struggled. Baby Shimmer would phase uncontrollably \- disappearing during feedings, flickering through her crib, sometimes vanishing for terrifying minutes before reappearing sobbing and gasping.

It was **Jasper Quickfingers**, the gnome juggler, who found the solution. He’d been performing in a market town when an old tiefling merchant pulled him aside. “I sense something unusual about your cavalcade. A child who exists between. I have something that might help.”

The amulet was unassuming \- a simple silver disc on a chain, inscribed with symbols that seemed to shift when you weren’t looking directly at them. “The child must never remove it. It’s for her protection. From what, I cannot say, but trust me \- she needs this.” Jasper paid every coin he had, plus a promise of three future favors.

When they placed the amulet around baby Shimmer’s neck, the change was immediate. She solidified completely, her purple eyes focusing clearly, her tiny hand grasping Cornelius’s finger with actual, constant pressure.

### **The Boy from the Deep (818 P.D.)**

When Shimmer was six, the Cavalcade rescued another unusual child. **Sokka of the Cerulean Depths** had been found half-drowned in a tide pool near Nicodranas, abandoned for the “curse” of his siren blood. The triton boy, barely seven, could make sounds that weren’t quite words and weren’t quite songs.

Shimmer was the first person unaffected by his voice’s compulsion. “You sound like music looks when I play it,” she told him matter-of-factly, and somehow, he understood. From that day forward, they were inseparable.

### **Musical Awakening (820-825 P.D.)**

By age eight, Shimmer’s bardic abilities manifested. Rhythms tapped on tables made flowers turn toward her. Jasper carved her first drumsticks from weirwood \- when she first used them, the sound rippled visually through the air in purple and silver waves. “It’s like your music exists in two places at once,” Jasper marveled.

## **Part II: The Flowering Years (825-834 P.D.)**

Puberty brought changes Shimmer delighted in. Her horns grew into elegant curves like Cornelius’s, purple fading to white at the tips. Her cosmic freckles became more pronounced, actually twinkling in darkness like distant galaxies. At fourteen, she started decorating her horns with silver chains and bells Cornelius had given her \- they made soft, ethereal music when she moved.

By eighteen, Shimmer and Sokka had become the Cavalcade’s star attraction. Their signature performance, “**The Ballad of Two Worlds**,” was pure theater \- a story about souls bound across dimensions, reaching for one another no matter what barriers divided them. The audience always read it as romance. To Shimmer and Sokka, it was about something else: the kind of bond that outlasts distance.

In **Deastok**, a Cerberus Assembly mage offered to buy Shimmer’s contract, fascinated by her visual magic. Cornelius’s polite refusal was firm: “The Cavalcade doesn’t sell family. Good day to you.” The ban from the city was worth it.

By twenty-one, Shimmer and Sokka’s bond was unshakable. Creative, emotional, soul-deep \- though never romantic. They exchanged promise gifts \- Sokka gave her his bulette tooth necklace, she gave him a chord woven from her eternally-regenerating hair. *I am yours, and you are mine. We are family beyond family.*

## **Part III: The Shattering (835 P.D.)**

### **The Last Perfect Show (15th of Quen’pillar, Morning)**

The day was beautiful. They were set up outside **Blumenthal**, the spring air perfect for an outdoor show. Shimmer and Sokka performed “The Shepherd’s Dream,” their newest piece. Her drums created the cosmos above the audience while his voice became the shepherd’s wonder. During the performance, she felt a strange pulling sensation \- like an echo of her music was going somewhere else \- but it passed quickly.

Cornelius was crying happy tears at the end. Seraphina captured the moment in a crystal, and they all hugged on stage. “I love you all so much,” Shimmer said, her amulet warm against her throat. “We love you too, little star,” Seraphina replied. It was perfect. Everything was perfect.

### **The Raid (15th of Quen’pillar, Dusk)**

The attackers came at dusk with military precision. Over two dozen figures in dark cloaks, targeting specific performers.

Shimmer was in her wagon with Sokka, laughing about a child who had tried to catch the visual butterflies her drums had created, when **Whisper** burst through the door in a panic, gesturing frantically \- danger, flee, now\!

The attack was chaos. Shimmer ran out to find her family under assault. She saw Jasper fall, a blade through his chest, and ran to him. “Play… play for them all, little star,” he gasped. “Make the worlds dance…” He died in her arms.

Sokka was grabbed from behind, a collar suppressing his siren song. Shimmer reached for him \- her anchor, her other half \- and then something cracked inside her. Her amulet burned like fire, and for one impossible moment, she saw double.

She saw the **Ethereal Plane** \- a gray echo of the same scene, where shadows watched. She couldn’t understand it, couldn’t process it, and then the amulet flared and pulled her fully back. But it was too late. She watched, helpless, as they dragged Sokka away. She saw **Grax** fall, Whisper vanish, **The Painted Lady** struck down. She held **Grandfather Oak** as he died. She saw **Maraleth**’s flames extinguished.

And Cornelius \- her father in all but blood \- took a cursed blade meant for Seraphina. The curse was unlike anything they had seen, resisting all healing.

### **The Aftermath**

When dawn broke, the Cavalcade was shattered. The dead were scattered, the living were traumatized, the kidnapped were gone. For Shimmer, the silence left behind was unbearable. Without Sokka, the world felt unbalanced, like music missing its rhythm. She couldn’t speak for three weeks. Not from fear of her nature, or even confusion about that strange double-vision. She couldn’t speak from grief.

When she finally found words, she said only: “I’m going to find them. I’m going to save Father. I’m going to bring Sokka home.” She didn’t question the amulet. She didn’t dwell on that impossible glimpse of two worlds. None of that mattered. What mattered was her family \- and the missing piece of her own soul that had been taken from her.

# **Sir Puddingfoot (John “Puddingfoot” Winters)**

**Race:** Geppettin (Plushie construction; made of fabric) \- **Class:** Blood Hunter 4 (Order of the Profane Soul, The Undead) \- **Background:** Archaeologist \- **Faith:** The Raven Queen (devoted) \- **Alignment:** Chaotic Good \- **Age:** The Raven Queen wishes without a fault (sheet phrasing) \- **Height:** 185 \- **Skin:** Warm pale \- **Eyes:** Red \- **Hair:** White \- **Size:** Small \- **AC:** 13 \- **HP:** 39 \- **Immunities:** Magical Sleep \- **Darkvision:** 60ft \- **STR 16 / DEX 12 / CON 14 / INT 19 / WIS 9 / CHA 8**

## **Part I: The Scholar’s Life (639-666 PD)**

### **The Man Before the Tragedy**

**John Marcus Winters** was born in 639 PD in the **Jrusar of Marquet**, the second son of a merchant family dealing in historical artifacts and curiosities. His father, **Marcus Winters**, had built a respectable business acquiring and selling pre-Calamity relics to wealthy collectors and academic institutions. His mother, **Cordelia Winters**, was a former scribe who kept the shop’s detailed catalogs and taught John to read using ancient texts.

From age five, John would sit cross-legged in his father’s shop, “**The Gilded Antiquity**,” carefully cleaning pottery shards with a soft brush while his father told him stories of the Age of Arcanum \- of floating cities that touched the clouds, of mages who walked as gods, and of the terrible Calamity that brought it all crashing down. His older brother, **Edmund**, showed no interest in dusty relics, preferring to study the family’s account books, but John lived and breathed history.

### **Education and Expertise**

At fourteen, John was accepted as an apprentice at the **Rexxentrum Historical Society**, a modest institution overshadowed by the Soltryce Academy but respected for its methodical approach to archaeological preservation. His mentor, **Professor Aldric Umholtz**, was a 200-year-old dwarf who had personally cataloged over three thousand Calamity artifacts. Under Umholtz’s exacting tutelage, John learned proper excavation techniques that preserved even the most fragile materials; how to date artifacts using stratigraphic analysis; and ancient languages including Old Draconic, Pre-Calamity Common, and fragments of Aeoran.

### **Love Amid the Ruins**

It was during his research in Zadash that John met **Serena Vess**, a half-elf cartographer with wild auburn hair and eyes the color of aged parchment. Serena worked as a freelance map-maker, selling her services to expeditions brave enough to venture into unmapped territories. She had approached John after attending one of his lectures on “Theoretical Ley Line Disruption Patterns Post-Calamity,” correcting an error in his geographical assumptions with a confident smile.

Their courtship took place in libraries and excavation sites. They married in 663 PD in a small ceremony at the **Temple of the Knowing Mistress**, with Professor Umholtz serving as John’s best man and nearly crying into his enormous beard.

### **The Birth of Elara**

**Elara Marie Winters** was born on a spring morning in 664 PD, arriving two weeks early while John was in the middle of presenting his findings on **Avalir** to the Historical Society. He rushed from the podium mid-sentence when a messenger brought news of Serena’s labor.

Elara was a curious child from the start, with her father’s intense focus and her mother’s fearless nature. By age one, she was attempting to “help” organize John’s artifact collection. By age two, she had memorized the names of a dozen ancient cities and would recite them as a bedtime song.

### **The Precious Bear**

For Elara’s second birthday, Serena’s mother, **Thessaly Vess**, traveled from Rosohna to meet her granddaughter. The family rarely discussed Thessaly’s connections to the Kryn Dynasty \- it was a complicated family history. She brought a magnificent gift: a pristine white teddy bear crafted by the finest Kryn toymakers.

The bear was a work of art: pure white fabric that shimmered in candlelight; button eyes of polished jet; stuffed with shimmerbird down; small silver bells sewn into its paws; a tiny blue silk bow around its neck. Elara named it “**Mister Snow**” and became inseparable from it.

## **Part II: The Expedition to Doom (666 PD)**

By early 666 PD, John had gathered enough evidence to convince the **Adventurer’s Coalition of Nicodranas** to fund a full expedition to the **Shattered Teeth**. The team included scholars from the Cobalt Soul archives, clerics of the Knowing Mistress, healers from the Temple of the Everlight, sailors of **The Stellar Crown** under **Captain Jeremiah “Blackwater” Morse**, and a contingent of guards led by **Lieutenant Commander Branna Ironfoot**. Among them: **Brother Matthias Grey** (cleric of the Knowing Mistress) and **Elias Windwick** (John’s eager young student).

### **The Mister Snow Repair**

During the voyage, an accident left Mister Snow torn. John spent that entire night below deck, carefully sewing the bear back together using fabric from his own black academic robes \- the ones awarded to him when he’d received his doctorate. The result was peculiar but charming: Mister Snow was now half white, half black, split perfectly down the middle. One of the jet button eyes had been replaced with a button from John’s robe \- this one made of polished horn that gave the bear an oddly asymmetrical, almost rakish appearance.

“Look, little star,” John told Elara, “Mister Snow is even more special now. He’s been on an adventure and has the scars to prove it. He’s part of Papa now too.” Elara loved it even more, declaring Mister Snow “the bravest bear in the whole world.”

### **Establishing Base Camp on Rumblecrest Island**

They arrived at **Rumblecrest Island** on the 15th of Misuthar, 666 PD. The island was roughly three miles across, covered in dense jungle. Ancient stone fragments jutted from the vegetation like broken teeth. Base camp was established on a cleared plateau on the eastern shore.

On the eighth day, the excavation team made the discovery: a stone passage leading down into the island’s heart, covered in a mixture of Celestial and Primordial script. “**This isn’t just a building,**” Professor Dunwich whispered. “**This is a piece of the Meridian Labyrinth \- part of Avalir’s actual magical infrastructure.**” The passage opened into a vast underground chamber partially flooded with seawater, with crystalline pillars that spiraled impossibly upward and walls that showed star-filled voids instead of stone.

### **The Final Morning**

On the morning of the 23rd of Misuthar, John kissed Serena and Elara goodbye. “Papa will be back before sunset,” he promised. Elara made him kiss Mister Snow too, “for extra luck.” The research team descended: John, Lyanna, Tobias, Korven, Melissa, and Matthias. Elias remained above. Two guards stayed at the entrance.

What they found below was the **Eldritch Battery** \- an Avalir relic, a soul-devouring crystal. It consumed John and his entire research team. Above, only Serena, Elara, Elias, and part of the expedition staff escaped before the island’s corrupted wildlife and aberrations were unleashed.

## **Part III: The Binding**

Elara, three years old, clutching Mister Snow, prayed \- prayed to the Raven Queen, the Matron of Ravens, with a child’s pure desperate certainty \- that her papa not be lost. Elara’s prayer was heard. John’s dispersing soul was caught and bound into the bear half-sewn from his own academic robe.

For 169 years, John lived inside Mister Snow. Watching. Unable to move. Unable to speak. Unable to act.

## **Part IV: Watching Elara Grow (666-674 PD)**

The memorial service was held in Nicodranas a month after the disaster. A marble marker was placed in the Remembrance Garden. John watched his own funeral through button eyes, Elara holding him on her lap. He heard Professor Umholtz, now truly crying into his beard, call John “the son he never had.”

For eight years, John watched his daughter grow from a traumatized toddler into a resilient child. Age 3: she insisted on setting a place for Mister Snow at every meal. Age 4: started formal education; she brought Mister Snow to every lesson. Age 5: defended Mister Snow fiercely when other children mocked her, giving a boy named Marcus a black eye when he tried to take the bear. Age 6: Serena began seeing **Willem Ashford**, a kind merchant. Age 7: Elara began leaving Mister Snow at home during school, but still told him everything each evening. Age 8: Serena married Willem. Elara held Mister Snow at the wedding, whispering, “It’s okay, Papa. Mama deserves to be happy.” Age 9: Elara’s half-brother Thomas was born. Age 10: Elara stopped talking to Mister Snow. Soon after, the bear was placed on a shelf.

## **Part V: The Years of Degradation (675-835 PD)**

For twenty-five years, Mister Snow existed in a trunk in darkness, John’s consciousness fracturing into elaborate fantasies. The trunk opened only occasionally. At 700 PD, an aged Elara \- 46 years old \- opened the trunk and gave Mister Snow to her own grandchild, **Marcus** (named for her grandfather). For fifty more years, the pattern repeated: Marcus, his daughter Julia (who sewed him a vest), Julia’s son Henry (who drew charcoal spectacles on the bear’s face that never washed out), various cousins. One child embroidered “Mr. Puddingfoot” on the left foot as a joke. The name stuck.

Eventually the family connection was lost. The bear was sold at an estate sale. For decades he passed through merchants \- **Giles Fairweather**, sold in Zadash, Rexxentrum, Port Damali, Deastok, Trostenwald. He witnessed a thief beaten in an alley. Mice nibbled his stuffing.

By 750 PD, he was no longer vintage \- just old, worn, unsellable. He was used as packing material; dropped and kicked; left in rain, sun, snow. John’s mind developed defense mechanisms: dissociation, cataloging, philosophy, hatred, apathy.

The Menagerie Coast Tours (750-760 PD). The Empire Routes (760-770 PD) \- including Vergesson sanatorium, used in “therapeutic sessions” for disturbed children. The Wastelander Period (770-780 PD) \- refugees, abandoned at a crossroads. The Collector’s Torment (780-790 PD) \- **Mistress Delphine Roswald** of Kamordah displayed him in a glass case among “cursed” toys for ten years. The Child Labor Years (790-800 PD) \- factory orphanage in Grimgolir.

The Carnival of Horrors (800-810 PD): **Morvek’s Magnificent Menagerie** incorporated him into “**The Haunted Nursery**.” Fortune teller **Madame Zelena** sensed something. “This one’s different. There’s something inside it. Something watching.” Her “exorcism” attempts only made things worse: burning sage, dousing in cheap wine, attempted séances.

The Scholar’s Irony (810-820 PD): A decade in the storage rooms of the **Alabaster Lyceum** in Zadash. Dr. Elmira Tross gave a lecture on the Shattered Teeth expedition, getting crucial details wrong, claiming John Winters had been “reckless and underprepared.” John seethed in silent fury.

The Final Merchant (820-835 PD): Sold to **Barnabas Bleek**, a traveling merchant of “curiosities and remnants.” Bleek kept Mister Puddingfoot as bulk filler. Through Xhorhas, along the Amber Road, into the Greying Wildlands. Finally to **Yrrosa**.

## **Part VI: The Cesspit of Yrrosa (833-835 PD)**

Yrrosa was a town that should have been abandoned decades ago. Mines ran dry in 798 PD. Trade routes shifted in 810 PD. Population dwindled from 3,000 to barely 400\. Bleek opened “**Treasures and Trinkets**” in 833 PD. By 835 PD, he was nearly bankrupt. Mister Puddingfoot sat in the window, sun-faded and water-stained, with a price tag that had been marked down seventeen times until it just read “Make Offer.”

### **The Day Everything Changed \- 23rd of Quen’pillar, 835 PD**

Bleek made a decision. He gathered up the absolute worst merchandise. Mr. Puddingfoot was grabbed roughly, stuffed into a sack with broken pottery and moldy shoes, and hauled to the alley behind the shop. Bleek threw the sack into the garbage heap. Mr. Puddingfoot landed face-up, half-buried in rotting vegetable matter, staring at a grey sky that promised rain but never delivered.

### **The Creature**

John had been in the garbage for perhaps an hour when he heard it \- a sound between a mewl and a whimper. Through his limited field of vision, he saw something that defied classification: roughly cat-sized but with proportions all wrong, covered in purple fur that seemed weak and sickly, wearing a **diamond-shaped collar**. It was clearly in pain.

### **The Children’s Cruelty**

Three boys emerged from behind a broken cart, laughing:

* **Tam Griggs (12):** ringleader, son of a failed miner turned meaner. Carried a sharpened stick.

* **Willem Mudge (11):** small but vicious, compensating for size with cruelty.

* **Boris Creel (10):** the follower, already regretting this but too afraid to stop.

“Look at it twitch\!” Tam poked the creature, drawing purple blood. “Bet it’s worth something to the right buyer. Magical beast parts fetch good coin.” The creature made a sound that was distinctly words, though no human language: “*Vash’tal neer. Vash’tal neer.*” (Please no. Please no.)

### **The Collar’s Flight**

Tam grabbed for the collar. “This’ll be worth something\!” The moment he touched it, the collar flared with light, burning his hand. He jerked back with a curse, and the collar flew off the creature’s neck, arcing through the air in an impossible trajectory, landing directly on the garbage heap. **It hit Mr. Puddingfoot square in the chest.**

### **The Voice**

Instantly, John heard something he hadn’t experienced in 169 years \- another consciousness touching his: “**Help me.**”

“I can’t. I can’t move. I can’t speak. I can’t do anything. And you can’t actually hear me.”

“I hear you perfectly. Help me.”

“What… what are you?”

“**I am a Friend, a Contract, a chance. That vessel dies. These children are crude but effective. Help me, and I will help you.**”

“How?\! I’m nothing\! I’m fabric and stuffing\! I’m garbage in garbage\!”

“You are a soul in a vessel. I can change the nature of the vessel. But only if we agree. Only if we bind.”

### **The Decision**

John thought of 169 years of helplessness. Of watching his daughter grow old without him. Of his wife remarrying. Of being forgotten, discarded, made into nothing. Of every moment he couldn’t act, couldn’t speak, couldn’t scream.

“**Yes. Whatever you are. Whatever the cost. YES.**”

“Then we are agreed. We are **BOUND**.”

## **Part VII: The Awakening of Mr. Puddingfoot**

The collar transformed into a diamond-shaped necklace around the neck of Mr. Puddingfoot’s fabric. John felt the power flooding through him \- not healing, not restoring, but changing the fundamental nature of what he was. Joints forming where none had been. A mouth developing behind the sewn smile. Internal structures that shouldn’t exist in a stuffed bear. Blood \- actual blood, black-red and impossible \- beginning to flow. The ability to MOVE.

His first motion in 169 years was to sit up.

Hearing the movement noise, the three boys spun around to see a teddy bear, half-black and half-white, standing in the garbage. Its mismatched button eyes gleamed with inner light. Around its neck, the diamond collar flickered like a heat mirage. Mr. Puddingfoot bent down, picked up a rusted metal rod from the garbage, and pointed it at the boys like a sword. “**Booga. Booga. BOOGA\!**”

It should have been ridiculous. A two-and-a-half-foot teddy bear making nonsense sounds. But the way he said it, the way shadows seemed to gather around him, the way his button eyes reflected something vast and hungry… Willem wet himself. Boris ran immediately, sobbing. Tam tried to maintain bravado for exactly three seconds before his nerve broke. “It’s a demon\!”

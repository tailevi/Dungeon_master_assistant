### **Appearance**

Almost mistaken for a human child at first glance, her fierce eyes convey her resolve. Always opting for black clothing, Fand keeps to the shadows, eavesdropping or keeping a sharp eye for opportunity or risk. Her pointy ears hint at some obscure ancestry, perhaps elvish or gnomish.

### **Backstory**

Fand "The Hand" Glimmers was born and raised in the lively halfling village of Hupperdook, known for its vibrant festivals and talented artisans. Both parents worked in a Gnomish tinker factory, trying to make an honest living and get their only daughter decent education. However, from a young age, Fand displayed an exceptional talent for sleight of hand and pickpocketing. When her mother caught her misbehaving she used to berate her, typically calling her by her full, hated, name \- **Fandarella\!** Her skills caught the attention of Jakser, a charismatic but ruthless halfling rogue who led a notorious gang of thieves.  
Jakser took Fand under his wing, becoming a father figure to the young halfling. He honed her natural abilities and taught her the art of infiltration, lockpicking, and deception. Under his guidance, Fand became an invaluable member of the gang, earning the nickname "The Hand" for her unparalleled dexterity.  
However, as Fand grew older, she began to manifest an innate psychic ability that allowed her to project her willpower onto others. At random times, she would inadvertently cause those around her to hear whispers of her thoughts or subtly influence their actions. When Jakser discovered this power, he became obsessed with harnessing it for his own gain.  
Jakser pushed Fand to use her psychic abilities during heists, despite her reluctance and lack of control. During a particularly challenging job, Fand's powers surged, causing chaos among the gang members and nearly exposing their location to the authorities. Furious and fearful, Jakser turned on Fand, accusing her of betrayal and threatening to turn her over to the town guards.  
Heartbroken by Jakser's betrayal and fearing for her life, Fand fled Hupperdook, leaving behind both her real and her newly found family. She traveled for weeks, using her roguish skills to survive, until she finally reached the sprawling city of Yrossa.  
In Yrrosa, Fand found herself alone and desperate, with the increasing presence of the mysterious voices in her head adding stress to her already wretched existence. As she wandered the unfamiliar streets, her paranoia grew. Suddenly, she spotted hooded figures in the crowd, their faces obscured. Fear gripped her heart as her mind raced \- could these be Jakser's agents, come to drag her back to her former life?  
Panic rising, Fand's eyes darted around frantically, searching for an escape. Her gaze fell upon the weathered sign of an inn \- The Salty Seahorse. Without a second thought, she bolted towards it, her small halfling frame weaving through the crowd with practiced ease.  
She burst through the door of The Salty Seahorse, the din of the tavern momentarily masking the sound of her ragged breathing. Fand quickly sought a shadowy corner, her back pressed against the wall, her eyes fixed on the entrance. As she huddled there, her heart pounding, she wondered if she would ever truly escape her past or find a place to belong. For now, though, all she could do was hide and hope that the shadows of her former life wouldn't find her in this new town of Yrrosa.

### **Backstory**

**Origins and Early Years.** In the shadowy underbelly of Exandria's criminal world, a goblin named Bozo stumbled upon a peculiar treasure \- a fox-eared toddler, alone and abandoned. Around her neck hung a delicate silver necklace, its crescent moon pendant concealing two secrets: a miniature portrait of the child and the name "Aiko" etched in flowing script. This necklace would become Aiko's only link to her mysterious past.  
Bozo, a self-proclaimed merchant with a penchant for the illegal, saw potential in the unique child. He took her in, not out of kindness, but as an investment. From her earliest years, Aiko was molded into Bozo's perfect tool, trained in the arts of thievery, con artistry, and performance.  
As Aiko grew, so did her talents. Her natural charm, combined with her exotic appearance, made her an ideal distraction and lure. Bozo paraded her through seedy taverns and criminal haunts, using her skills to curry favor with the underworld. Aiko learned to dance with hypnotic grace, her movements accentuated by her fluffy tail. Her voice, sweet and alluring, could enchant even the most hardened criminals.  
Despite the harsh realities of her life, Aiko couldn't help but yearn for genuine affection. Watching families in the streets, she'd wonder about her own origins, tracing the letters of her name on her hidden necklace and dreaming of parents who might have loved her.  
**The Terrible Trio.** When Aiko turned five, Bozo expanded his "family." He acquired two more child slaves, each as unique and exotic as Aiko:  
**Lera** (also known as Lira in earlier records): A young changeling girl with opalescent skin and eyes that shifted color with her mood. Her malleable features made her an ideal infiltrator and impersonator.  
**Sokka**: A sylph boy with translucent, wind-touched skin and hair that seemed to float as if underwater. His otherworldly grace made him a captivating acrobat and pickpocket.  
The three children formed a tight bond, finding solace in each other amidst their shared misery. Each of them, plucked from different realms or planes, shared the burden of being outsiders in this world. Bozo worked them relentlessly, teaching them to pickpocket, con, and perform for his benefit, always emphasizing how their unique appearances made them valuable "assets."  
Despite Lera's changeling abilities allowing her to assume any form, Aiko always had a way to identify her friend. In moments of stress or concentration, Lera would absently trace a circular pattern on her palm with her thumb. This subtle gesture, a remnant of a soothing habit from her earliest memories, became an unconscious tell. Aiko, hyper-aware of her friend's mannerisms after years together, could always recognize Lera by this small movement, no matter what form she took. Lera remained oblivious to this habit, believing her transformations to be flawless. This secret knowledge became a small comfort to Aiko, a hidden connection to her friend amidst the chaos of their lives.  
Bozo's operation was supported by a crew of unsavory characters:

* **Groknak** \- A brutal ogre enforcer, known for his short temper and cruel punishments.  
* **Sper** \- A Kryn assassin who handled Bozo's more delicate matters.  
* **Fiztik** \- A hobgoblin alchemist who concocted potions to keep the children compliant when needed.

**The Failed Escape.** As the children approached their teens, Sokka's rebellious spirit grew. At 14, he hatched a plan for all three to escape. Aiko, ever cautious, tried to dissuade him, fearing the consequences. But Sokka was determined.  
The attempt was a disaster. Groknak caught Aiko and Lera before they could get far. Sokka, however, managed to evade capture for three days, using his sylph abilities to stay just out of reach. When Bozo's men finally dragged him back, the goblin decided to make an example of him.  
Before Aiko and Lera's horrified eyes, Groknak beat Sokka to death.  
Her fox-like features became more pronounced, and her first tail emerged fully on her 18th birthday.  
**The Yrrosa Job.** At 20, Bozo saw an opportunity for a big score. He'd heard rumors of a rare and valuable fruit called the Dyolet, with potent magical properties. His sources indicated that a gnoll bandit group led by Verok Bloodhowl was planning to intercept a shipment of these fruits bound for Yrrosa.  
Bozo's plan was twofold: Have Aiko infiltrate the caravan as a merchant to locate the fruits. If the gnolls succeeded, have Aiko pose as a hostage to gather intel from inside.  
Aiko, disguised as a young merchant named "Lumi," joined the caravan at the Amber Crossroads. For two weeks, she traveled with the group, building trust and searching for the Dyolet fruits. She befriended several fellow travelers:

* **Old Garen** \- A kindly dwarf merchant who took "Lumi" under his wing.  
* **Sera** \- A human guard with a soft spot for the seemingly naive young merchant.  
* **Tik-Tik** \- A curious kenku chronicler documenting trade routes.

Just as Aiko was starting to enjoy the semblance of a normal life, disaster struck. Verok's gnolls ambushed the caravan, slaughtering many and taking the rest hostage. Aiko allowed herself to be captured, beginning the most harrowing chapter of her mission.  
**Weeks of Torment.** For three grueling weeks, Aiko endured the brutality of Verok's camp. The gnolls were cruel, subjecting their captives to beatings, starvation, and psychological torture. Aiko struggled to maintain her cover while also trying to comfort her fellow hostages.  
Bozo would visit the camp under the guise of a slave trader, ostensibly to negotiate with Verok. During these visits, he'd secretly provide Aiko with minimal sustenance and gather her intel. To maintain her cover, Bozo would loudly haggle over Aiko's price, pretending to be interested in purchasing her to "keep her safe."  
Throughout this ordeal, Aiko's resolve was tested. She formed a tight bond with the survivors from the caravan, particularly:

* **Mira** \- A young elf girl separated from her parents during the attack.  
* **Brom** \- An elderly human scholar who maintained hope through stories and songs.

Aiko found herself torn between her mission for Bozo and her growing desire to truly help these people.  
**The Rescue and New Beginning.** Just as hope seemed lost, salvation arrived in the form of a group of adventurers. They stormed the gnoll camp, cutting down Verok and his bandits. In the chaos, Bozo vanished, leaving Aiko with hurried instructions to infiltrate the adventuring party and continue the search for the Dyolet fruits.  
As Aiko joins the party under the guise of a grateful rescued hostage, she faces a moral crossroads. For the first time, she has a chance at freedom and doing genuine good. Yet, the shadow of Bozo's influence and the fear for Lera's safety hang over her.

### **Backstory**

I awoke with a start, my consciousness flooding into being amidst the hum of rebooting systems, echoing unnervingly in the vast, dark space around me. As my visual sensors came online, the shadowy outlines of a colossal underground facility unfolded before me, filled with rows upon rows of stasis fields. Each held an automaton like myself, yet unlike me, many were damaged, their forms distorted and broken by what seemed like the cruel hand of natural disasters.  
Stepping gingerly from my pod, I scanned the eerie silence, my internal diagnostics reporting errors I could not comprehend. A red warning blinked incessantly within my vision, a stark reminder of a critical core malfunction. My core, the very source of my life and function, was compromised, emitting sporadic bursts of energy that caused my frame to shudder uncontrollably. This uncontrolled power was terrifying; I was a creature of precision and purpose, yet here I stood, erratic and unsure.  
Fear gripped me, a primal sensation that seemed out of place in my mechanical being. I was alone, utterly alone, in a crypt of my fallen kin, with no memory of who I was or what I was supposed to protect. The realization that I had failed in my primary directive \- to guard something of importance \- was suffocating, yet I could not recall what or whom I was meant to safeguard.  
My gaze fell upon a control panel near my pod, coated in dust and debris. As I approached, a flickering screen displayed a snippet of information before fading out: "G.U.A.R.D P2RX2 Magen Series 9." The name 'Magen' stuck with me, a name I adopted as my own, clinging to it like a lifeline in the storm of my uncertainty. It was the only link I had to a past now lost.  
With each hesitant step through the corridors of this forsaken facility, the fear of the unknown battled with the emerging bursts of unbridled power from my damaged core. I was powerful yet vulnerable, created to protect yet incapable of even remembering my mission. The facility stretched out like a labyrinth, a tomb of technology where I wandered, driven by the faint hope that beyond this darkness, I might find the answers I sought and perhaps a way to mend the broken fragments of my existence.  
Emerging from the depths of the underground facility, I found myself on the precipice of a vast quarry. My sensors adjusted to the bright light, revealing the harsh activities below. Soldiers of the empire, clad in their regimented uniforms, were dismantling automatons like me, stripping them of their parts and casting them into great furnaces. The sight triggered a primal warning within my systems: these people were a threat, not just to me, but to any like me. My purpose \- though unclear \- indeed wasn't to end up as molten scrap.  
Driven by a surge of urgency, I retreated swiftly into the surrounding forest, using the dense foliage as cover. My damaged core occasionally pulsed with raw power, aiding my escape but also reminding me of my vulnerability. For two days, I moved ceaselessly, avoiding any paths or signs of human activity, driven by an instinctive need to survive and fulfill my yet-unknown directive.  
On the morning of the third day, a distressing sound pierced the usual stillness of the woods \- the growls and snarls of wolves and the shouts of humans. Peering through the underbrush, I observed a trader caravan under attack. The sight of the beleaguered traders struggling against the onslaught of nature ignited something within me. A directive took hold, one clear and undeniable in its demand: protect.  
Without a second thought, I charged into the fray, my metallic limbs swinging with devastating accuracy, driving the wolves back with bursts of force that even I hadn't fully realized I possessed. The traders, shocked yet grateful, watched as I dispatched their attackers.  
Among them was a diminutive figure, a goblin with sharp eyes and a cunning smile. He introduced himself as **Bozo**, the merchant leading the caravan. His gaze was calculating, taking in my formidable form and the ease with which I had handled the threat. When he asked who I was and what I was doing, my response was simple yet profound to my understanding of myself: "I am Magen, and I am meant to defend. I need to find the one I am supposed to protect."  
Bozo's eyes lit up with what I now understand was opportunistic cunning. He offered a deal: in exchange for protection and labor, he would help me discover my true purpose. Naively, I agreed, desperate for any lead to reconnect me with my original directive.  
Together, we traveled to Yarosa, a bustling city where my appearance would undoubtedly draw unwanted attention. Bozo provided me with a hood and cloak, advising me to keep my head down. The fabric felt foreign against my metallic skin, but it served its purpose, shielding me from the curious eyes of city guards and citizens alike.  
Now, I work for Bozo, performing tasks that are often menial and sometimes questionable. Each day, I hope these tasks will lead me closer to discovering the entity I am sworn to protect. Bozo assures me that every job completed is a step towards that goal, though I remain cautious of his intentions. Deep within, my core pulses \- not just with the wild energy of its damage but with the persistent drive of my primary directive. I must protect, and I will find what I was made to guard.

### **Backstory \- A Tale of Wild Magic in Exandria**

In the harsh landscape of the Hellcatch Valley, nestled within the dangerous city of Bassuras, Neilo's life began under uncertain circumstances. Abandoned as an infant, his origins and parentage remain a mystery. He was left on the doorstep of a rundown tavern known as "The Tarnished Scale," a dim refuge amidst the chaos of Bassuras.  
The tavern's owner, a tough but kind-hearted half-orc woman named **Grenda**, took pity on the infant and decided to raise him as best she could. The Tarnished Scale became Neilo's home, and its eclectic mix of patrons his makeshift family.

### **Backstory**

**Part One: The Grave.** She crawled from the earth five years ago. A shallow grave in a vineyard outside Kamordah. Soil parting around fingers that shouldn't move. Lungs filling with air they didn't need.  
She didn't know her name. Didn't know where she was. Didn't know why her hands were grey-beige and wrong, why her hair wasn't hair but black feathers matted with grave dirt, why her face felt sharpened into something corvid when she touched it.  
She only knew three things:

* The phantom weight of a veil across her face.  
* The echo of vows she couldn't recall speaking.  
* And a ring. Simple silver. Pressing against her chest on a cord she didn't remember tying.

When she opened her eyes, she saw nothing. Blind.  
On her back \- three vertical runes carved deep into flesh that should have scarred but hadn't. Brands. Old ones. She found them by touch, tracing the grooves with trembling fingers. She didn't know what they meant.  
She walked for three days. Blind. Silent. Following the sound of civilization until she collapsed outside Kamordah's walls.  
**Part Two: What She Is Without a Face.** Those first hours were the worst.  
Without sight, without memory, without understanding \- she was nothing but sensation and terror. Fragments of images crashed through her mind like waves: lanterns swaying in darkness, hands clasped in hers, water closing over her head, a voice calling a name she couldn't quite hear.  
She screamed. She wept. She clawed at her own face, trying to understand why it felt wrong, why the skin didn't match the shape she remembered. Her breath came in ragged gasps. Her heart \- did she have a heart? \- raced with panic that had no end and no relief.  
This is what she is without a mask: chaos. Anxiety that eats itself. Sorrow so deep it has no bottom. A soul drowning in fragments of a life it can't remember, reaching for something solid and finding only water.  
She curled against the cold stone of Kamordah's outer wall and waited to die. Again. Properly this time.  
**Part Three: The Matron's Gift.** A priest of the Raven Queen found her at dawn.  
**Brother Vasek** served at a small shrine to the Matron of Ravens on the outskirts of Kamordah \- a modest place where travelers paid respects before journeys and families sought blessings for their departed. He discovered the blind elf-woman curled against the shrine's doorstep, naked and covered in grave dirt, breathing but unresponsive.  
He brought her inside. Cleaned her wounds. Asked her questions she couldn't answer.  
What struck him wasn't her condition \- he'd seen the desperate and broken before. It was her stillness between episodes of panic. She would scream and weep, then suddenly go quiet, head tilted as if listening to something far away. Then the panic would return, worse than before.  
When he examined her back and saw the brands, he sent word to Zadash.  
Three days later, a senior priestess arrived from the temple in the Signet Wall \- **Mother Irina Valdros**, old and sharp-eyed, who had served the Matron for fifty years. She studied the blind woman. Studied the runes. Asked questions in languages living and dead.  
The woman answered none of them. She couldn't stop shaking.  
"She's not undead," Mother Irina concluded. "But she's not entirely alive either. Her soul is... displaced. Wearing flesh that isn't hers. The Matron should detest such a thing."  
"Should?" Brother Vasek asked.  
"The goddess hates those who pervert the passage between life and death. Necromancers. Liches. Those who refuse to move on." Mother Irina touched the woman's face, tilting it toward the candlelight. "But this one didn't choose her condition. Her soul can't move on. It's trapped. Anchored to something she can't remember."  
They brought her to Zadash. To the temple proper. To the blood pool where the faithful communed with their goddess.  
The pool was cold. Cold enough to stop a living heart.  
They lowered her in \- naked, blind, still trembling \- and the blood closed over her head like a burial shroud. She didn't struggle. Didn't breathe. Just sank into the red darkness and waited for whatever came next.  
The Matron of Ravens came.  
Not in full manifestation \- even the goddess's attention is a precious thing \- but enough. A presence in the blood. A whisper against the soul.  
She felt herself seen. Examined. Judged.  
*You are an aberration. A soul in borrowed flesh. Undeath without corruption. A thread of fate so tangled I cannot see its end.*  
She had no words to offer. Only the fragments: lanterns in darkness, hands clasped in hers, the weight of a ring against her heart.  
*You should not exist. And yet...*  
A pause. In that pause, something like curiosity.  
*You intrigue me, broken thing. Your soul yearns to move on but cannot. Your fate is hidden even from my sight. I do not know if you are an affront to my domain or a mystery worth unraveling.*  
The presence withdrew. But something remained in the blood with her \- an object pressing against her palm.  
When they pulled her from the pool, she was clutching a mask.  
**Part Four: The Karasu.** A half-mask carved from bone. Ancient. White as moonlight. Shaped like a raven's skull with hollow eye sockets that seemed to drink the candlelight.  
She lifted the mask to her face \- acting on instinct she didn't understand \- and the bone fit. Molded to her features as if carved for her alone.  
And through the hollow sockets, she could see.  
The world appeared in muted greys and whites. The temple. The blood pool. The faces of the priests staring at her with expressions that registered as data rather than emotion.  
Everything washed of color. Of warmth. Of feeling.  
The panic stopped. The grief stopped. The drowning sensation of being lost in fragments of memory \- it didn't disappear, but it receded. Became quiet. Became manageable. Like watching a storm through thick glass instead of standing in the rain.  
With the mask on, she was calm. Functional. Empty in a way that felt like mercy.  
"The Matron has claimed her," Mother Irina said finally. "Or perhaps merely marked her for observation. Either way, she is no longer our responsibility to judge."  
They let her go. What else could they do? The goddess herself had intervened.  
She walked out of the temple wearing the bone mask, seeing the world for the first time in muted monochrome, feeling nothing at all.  
It was the greatest relief she had ever known.  
**Part Five: The Nature of the Masks.** The Karasu showed her a truth about herself: masks could give her selves.  
Not just sight. Not just function. The bone mask imposed a personality \- cold, detached, utterly without empathy. When she wore it, she became something different. Something that could navigate a world she otherwise couldn't perceive.  
She called herself **Yurei**. It meant "ghost" in a language she didn't remember learning.  
Over the following months, she discovered that other masks worked similarly. Not because of any special property in the masks themselves \- but because masks carry echoes. The personality of those who wore them, who poured emotion and identity into carved wood and painted faces. When she dons a mask with history, she channels fragments of its previous owner.  
Most people feel nothing when they wear a theatrical mask. For her, with her displaced soul and hollow sense of self, the echoes fill her. Give her shape. Give her purpose.  
Each mask she wears creates a different version of her. A different life. A different set of relationships. She doesn't always remember what one self does while wearing another face.  
**Part Six: The Kamordah Years (Years 1-3).** She drifted around Kamordah for three years, building three separate lives without meaning to.  
*As Yurei* \- She took work that others wouldn't touch. Watching buildings through the night. Delivering packages that couldn't be opened. Standing in rooms where violence was about to happen and reporting afterward, with clinical precision, exactly what occurred.  
Her complete lack of emotional response made her valuable to people who needed witnesses without consciences. She observed murders without flinching. Counted wounds with academic interest. Noted time of death the way a scholar notes weather patterns.  
She made no friends. Only transactions.  
The bone mask was her default. Her safe place. When she felt the chaos rising, she pressed the Karasu to her face and let the emptiness swallow everything. She always returned to Yurei.  
*As Kimiko* \- She found the cat mask in a market stall, purchased from a Marquesian merchant who claimed it belonged to a legendary courtesan from the Menagerie Coast. The woman had worked the finest pleasure houses of Port Damali for thirty years, they said. Could read desire like others read books. Died wealthy, surrounded by admirers who'd have killed for her attention.  
When she put on the mask, the courtesan's hunger filled her. The world shifted. Colors appeared \- warm tones, golden and amber. She could see desire like heat rising from skin. She could read the architecture of want in the way people held their shoulders, tilted their heads, let their eyes linger.  
She called herself **Kimiko**. The name felt right in the mask's mouth. Kimiko found pleasure houses and gambling dens in Kamordah. The mask's previous owner had left strong echoes \- a hunger for wealth, attention, influence. Kimiko gathered secrets the way magpies gather shiny things, trading them for coin and favors.  
*As Momo* \- The tengu mask came from a shrine outside Kamordah, an old monument to a warrior-spirit that locals had stopped visiting generations ago. She found it half-buried in dead leaves, cracked and faded, clearly ancient. Red face, long nose, fierce expression \- a traditional tengu, top half only.  
The moment she put it on, something roared awake inside her. The world turned red-tinted and sharp. Her muscles felt stronger. Her reflexes faster. Joy \- actual joy \- flooded through her at the thought of violence.  
She called herself **Momo**. She laughed constantly. She sought out fighting rings in villages and crossroads taverns. The warrior-spirit in the mask craved combat like others crave food. She won some. Lost some. Made enemies without remembering their faces.  
The three lives existed in parallel, occasionally colliding in ways she didn't understand until later. Debts Momo incurred that Kimiko had to pay. Information Kimiko gathered that Yurei was hired to verify. Violence Yurei witnessed that Momo had caused.  
Eventually, the tangles in Kamordah grew too thick. Too many people asking too many questions about the strange women with the masks. She drifted toward Zadash, following rumors of bigger opportunities and darker secrets.  
**Part Seven: Zadash \- Yurei's Work (Years 3-5).** Zadash swallowed her whole.  
The city was large enough for three lives to exist without collision \- at first.  
Yurei found work in the shadows of the Outersteads. The same kind of work she'd done in Kamordah, but more of it. More violence to witness. More packages to deliver. More questions not to ask.  
She became known in certain circles as reliable. The skull-faced woman who watched and reported and never, ever reacted. Some people found her unsettling. Others found her useful. She didn't care either way. Caring required feeling.  
**Part Eight: Zadash \- Kimiko at the Triumph Chime.** The Siren's Silk welcomed Kimiko like a homecoming.  
The brothel occupied the fifth floor of the Triumph Chime \- an eight-story tower in the Tri-Spires that served as gambling hall, theater, and pleasure house all in one. The Siren's Silk catered to wealthy merchants, nobles, and those who preferred their pleasures wrapped in sophistication.  
Kimiko's mask and mystery made her an instant curiosity. The cat-faced woman who never showed her true face, who listened more than she spoke, who seemed to know what you wanted before you did.  
Madam **Cordelia Trask** recognized her value quickly. The establishment's proprietor was a shrewd businesswoman who understood that some clients wanted more than physical pleasure \- they wanted to be understood.  
Kimiko didn't service clients in the traditional sense. She entertained them. Conversation. Companionship. The illusion of intimacy without its complications. Wealthy men told her things they'd never tell their wives. Merchants confessed business dealings they'd deny under oath. Politicians whispered fears they couldn't voice in daylight.  
Kimiko remembered everything. Sold what was useful. Kept what might become useful later.  
Through her work, she obtained a pass to the Tri-Spires \- the exclusive district of Zadash separated from the common areas by magical barriers and Crownsguard checkpoints. The pass identified her as an employee of the Triumph Chime, granting her legitimate access to the wealthy district.  
She also befriended a performer there \- a charming bard from Marquet named **Ben Arnold** who worked the same floors, entertaining guests with music and stories. He was flirtatious with her, always trying to see beneath the mask, always curious about the mysterious cat-faced woman. She found his attention amusing. Harmless.  
He knew her only as Kimiko.  
*What Kimiko Overheard.* Six months ago, Kimiko was entertaining a nervous client in one of the private alcoves when she heard something she shouldn't have.  
The client was **Lord Kai Arness** \- a powerful noble with ties to the Dwendalian royal family, appointed to Zadash's Administrative Council. He visited the Siren's Silk regularly, but this night he wasn't seeking pleasure. He was meeting someone in the adjacent alcove, separated only by a silk curtain.  
Kimiko heard fragments:  
*"...the Retainer... I need to know who they are..."*  
*"...my nephew has gone too far... this servant of his..."*  
*"...if I could just find leverage... understand what this Retainer wants..."*  
The fear in Lord Arness's voice was unmistakable. This was a powerful man trying to investigate something that terrified him. Someone called "the Retainer." Someone connected to his nephew.  
Kimiko filed the information away. She didn't understand its significance \- it was just another secret among hundreds. But she remembered: Lord Arness. His nephew. The Retainer.  
What she didn't know was that Lord Arness had spotted her afterward. Had noticed the cat-masked woman who'd been in the adjacent alcove. Had wondered, later, exactly how much she'd heard.  
**Part Nine: Zadash \- Momo and the Krovniks.** The fighting pits in the Eastern Outersteads were run by a family called the **Krovniks**.  
**Bogdan Krovnik** controlled blood sport gambling throughout the district \- underground brawls in basements beneath taverns, where gold changed hands and blood stained the sawdust. He was a calculating man who saw fighters as entries on a ledger. His enforcers, **Ygor** and **Stas**, handled recruitment and "motivation."  
Momo didn't care about any of that. She just wanted to fight.  
She became a regular at the pits, drawn by the warrior-spirit in her mask. The crowd loved her \- the tengu-faced woman who laughed when she bled, who thanked opponents for good hits, who treated every match like a celebration of violence.  
The Krovniks noticed. Good fighters meant good betting. Good betting meant good money. Stas Krovnik, who ran the pits directly, approached her after her third win.  
"You're popular. The betting's heavy when you fight. We could use someone like you on the regular."  
Momo grinned behind her red mask. "You want me to fight MORE? Yes\! I accept\!"  
And so she became one of the Krovniks' regular fighters. Weeks of matches. Weeks of victories. Weeks of the crowd chanting for the demon-faced woman who seemed to genuinely love every moment of violence.  
Then came the request.  
"Next match," Stas told her before a particularly high-stakes bout. "You lose. Third round. Take a hit to the ribs, go down, stay down. We've got money riding on your opponent."  
Momo's grin froze. "You want me to... surrender? On purpose?"  
"It's business. You'll get a cut. Everyone wins."  
The warrior-spirit in the mask recoiled. Everything in her screamed against the suggestion.  
"Everyone except ME\!" Momo roared. "I don't LOSE on purpose\! What kind of warrior throws a fight?\!"  
She didn't throw the fight. She won decisively, breaking her opponent's arm in the second round when he tried to grapple her. The crowd went wild.  
The Krovniks lost a significant sum \- not just their own money, but money from people they couldn't afford to disappoint.  
That was three days ago.  
**Part Ten: The Present Crisis.**  
*Three Days Ago.* Momo refused to throw the fight. The Krovniks started hunting.  
*Two Days Ago.* There was someone in the crowd who'd been watching Momo for months.  
**Tobias Kern** was a young man from a merchant family who'd discovered the underground fights half a year ago. He became obsessed with the tengu-masked woman \- her ferocity, her joy, her complete lack of fear. He started attending every fight. Leaving flowers at the pit entrance. Writing letters to "the crimson warrior" describing his devotion.  
Two days ago, after Momo's disastrous refusal, Tobias followed her.  
He watched her duck into an alley. Watched her remove the tengu mask \- and put on a bone-white raven skull instead. He saw her entire demeanor change. The boisterous warrior became something cold and empty. She walked differently. Held herself differently. Even her feathered hair seemed to fall differently.  
Tobias doesn't understand what he witnessed. But he knows the tengu fighter and the skull-faced woman are the same person. He's been watching Yurei ever since. He's also been talking to people. Asking questions. The wrong questions, in the wrong ears.  
*Yesterday.* Word reached Stas Krovnik that their troublesome fighter might wear another face \- a skull mask. Krovnik enforcers began searching the Eastern Outersteads for anyone matching that description.  
*This Morning.* Yurei, with her characteristic detachment, assessed the situation. Multiple people searching for her. Heat from two directions. The Eastern Outersteads were compromised. She needed to disappear. Change her appearance. Find somewhere the Krovniks wouldn't look.  
The Halls of Erudition seemed like a reasonable destination. Academic. Quiet. Not the kind of place where pit fighting enforcers would think to search. She made her way to the Zauber Spire.  
**Part Eleven: The Halls of Erudition.** The Halls of Erudition occupied the lower floors of the Zauber Spire \- the great magical academy of Zadash, still showing signs of repair from the Kryn attack two weeks prior. Students in distinctive blue robes moved through the corridors, each robe bearing patterns that identified their field of study.  
Yurei observed. Calculated. Planned.  
She found what she needed near the dormitory wing: a cart loaded with laundry baskets, filled with robes destined for washing. A harried-looking student was pushing it, clearly resentful of the chore.  
Yurei approached. The student, a young human woman with ink-stained fingers: "You\! Why are you out of school uniform? You know it's a violation, right?"  
Yurei looked confused \- deadpan. The student looked up with obvious frustration. "If you don't want me to report you to the professors, you will take this cart and do my laundry duty. I have an exam to study for." She pushed the laundry cart into Yurei's hands. "It's just down to the washroom in the basement, they'll show you what to do." She was gone before Yurei could respond.  
Yurei looked at the cart full of blue robes. Pulled one out that seemed approximately her size \- agricultural studies, based on the wheat-sheaf pattern \- and copied it using her magical armor. The agricultural insignia was unremarkable. She looked like any other student doing a mundane task. Just like she had planned.  
She pushed the cart to an alleyway outside a classroom where a storage alcove is, and left it there. Then she walked into the main hall, riding the flow of students between classes, finding an empty seat in the back of a lecture hall where an elderly professor was droning about crop rotation techniques.  
She didn't belong here. She wasn't supposed to be doing anyone's laundry. She had no idea that a group of adventurers would soon be looking for students in these exact robes. She just knew she needed to disappear for a while. And the Tri-Spires, where the Krovniks couldn't easily follow, seemed like a good place to do it.  
She has a pass. Kimiko's pass. Hidden in her belongings. She just needs to survive long enough to use it.  
**Part Twelve: The Watchers.** There's one more thing.  
Sometimes, when Yurei walks the night streets, she notices them. Hooded figures in dark robes, watching from doorways or rooftops. They never approach. Never speak. Just... observe.  
The Raven Queen's priests haven't forgotten her. Mother Irina still sends reports, she assumes. Still wonders what the Matron sees in this broken creature. Still waits to see if the goddess's patience runs out.  
Yurei doesn't mind the watchers. In her emotionless state, their presence registers as data, nothing more. They follow. She walks. Eventually they leave. The pattern continues. She doesn't know if they're protecting her or preparing to destroy her.  
**Part Thirteen: The Ring.** Through everything \- the masks, the personalities, the tangled web of debts \- one thread remains constant: the ring.  
Simple silver. No gemstone. Inscribed with characters no scholar has been able to identify. She's shown it to antiquarians in Kamordah and Zadash alike. None of them recognized the script.  
"Ancient," they all agreed. "Very ancient. But I can't tell you what it says."  
She wears it on a cord against her chest, beneath whatever face she's showing the world. She was married. The ring proves it. But to whom? When? Where?  
The answers hide behind the wall in her mind where memories should be. She catches fragments sometimes \- lanterns in darkness, hands clasped in hers, the weight of a veil \- but nothing she can hold onto. She whispers words sometimes, in the darkness between masks. Testing names. Testing places. Searching for something that might unlock the door. Nothing has worked yet.
